# A Note on Process

The notes in this garden were written collaboratively. The ideas, experiences, observations and reflections are mine. The writing involved AI assistants.

This was not automation. It was dialogue. I provided context: course notes, readings, field observations, frameworks encountered during the week and my own reflections on them. AI helped shape that material into notes, clarifying, structuring, finding the right register and sometimes pushing back when something didn't hold together.

Every note went through iteration. What you read here is the result of that back-and-forth, not a generated output.

I find it appropriate to be transparent about this for two reasons. The first is honesty: these notes carry my voice and my thinking, but not my hand alone. The second is coherence. A garden about learning, methodology and the thoughtful use of technology should model what it advocates. Using AI as a thinking partner, not a shortcut, is part of what I mean when I say technology amplifies the human dimension of learning rather than replacing it.

If you are an educator curious about this process, I am happy to talk about it.

— Filipe Gama
