This edition ran as ten sessions across 25 contact hours, hosted by the Centro de Formação Terras de Santa Maria. The first seven were facilitated by Mário Cruz, the final three by Daniela Mascarenhas (Sessions 8–9) and Mário Cruz again (Session 10, the closing report session). The record below follows what was actually delivered, not the original 30h blueprint (see [[e-b-Learning Design for Basic and Secondary Education]] for that mapping).

## [[Session 1 — Distance Education Premises and Implications|Session 1 — Distance Education: Premises and Implications]]

5 March 2026 · Presencial, 18:00–21:00

- Twenty-first-century learning competencies and the "pronétariat"
- Critical thinking, the KWL chart, and Padlet
- From explanation pedagogy to connectivism, autonomy, and andragogy
- The "Creactical Skills" framework and the conceptual map of e-/b-/m-learning
- **Task 1** – Creative introduction on Padlet: build a KWL chart covering what you know, want to know, and (later) learned about e-/b-learning

## [[Session 2 — Virtual Learning Environments to Communities of Practice|Session 2 — e/b-learning: From Virtual Learning Environments to Communities of Practice]]

12 March 2026 · Online (synchronous), 18:00–20:30

- From platforms to people: virtual environments as communities of practice
- Discursive negotiation and the collaborative construction of meaning
- Communication tools as instruments of extended cognition
- **Task 2** – Virtual Escape Room with Genially: experience an existing escape room, then design an original one and submit it with a short reflection

## [[Session 3 — Learning Management Systems|Session 3 — Learning Management Systems: From Environments to Models]]

19 March 2026 · Online (asynchronous), 18:00–21:00

- LMS, CMS and PLE: definitions and Downes's critique
- Moodle and TalentLMS compared
- Collaborative learning and assessment within an LMS
- **Task 3** – Review quiz on Wayground: gamified knowledge check covering Sessions 1–3

## [[Session 4 — Developing an Online Course|Session 4 — Developing an Online Course: From Needs to Implementation]]

26 March 2026 · Online (asynchronous), 18:00–21:00

- Needs analysis, content delivery, and the administrative side of course design
- Microlearning as a structuring principle
- Sustaining engagement through gamified tasks
- **Task 4** – Narrated video presentation: record a 3–5 slide PowerPoint with narration and timings, structured around introducing a topic, explaining a concept, and proposing a follow-up activity

## [[Session 5 — Synchronous Communication Tools|Session 5 — e/b-learning Kit I: Synchronous Communication Tools]]

23 April 2026 · Online (synchronous), 18:00–20:30

- Zoom, Microsoft Teams, Google Meet, Skype, Internet Relay Chat
- Breakout rooms, screen sharing, live polling (Poll Everywhere, Anywhere Polls)
- Chat as a hybrid genre and dialogic negotiation in real time
- **Task 5** – Timed quiz: a 30-minute knowledge check on synchronous communication tools

## [[Session 6 — Asynchronous Communication Tools|Session 6 — e/b-learning Kit II: Asynchronous Communication Tools]]

30 April 2026 · Presencial, 18:00–21:00

- Email, forums, blogs and wikis
- Forums (general, Q&A), blogs as individual log files, wikis as collective spaces
- Building argument over time through linking and quoting
- **Task 6** – Structured debate with Kialo Edu: set up and run a class debate on a curricular question, then submit the link with a short reflection

## [[Session 7 — Production and Diffusion Tools|Session 7 — e/b-learning Kit III & IV: Production and Diffusion Tools]]

7 May 2026 · Presencial, 18:00–20:00

- Narrated PowerPoint and Genially for content production
- Kahoot, Quizizz, Mentimeter and TikTok for diffusion and feedback
- Quiz-building as an exercise in research and critical thinking (Schleicher, 2014)
- **Task 7** – Educational chatbot with Mizou: build a curriculum-themed chatbot, trial it with students, and submit the link with a short reflection

## [[Session 8 — Current and Emerging Trends|Session 8 — Current and Emerging Trends in e/b-Learning]]

14 May 2026 · Presencial, 18:00–20:00 · Daniela Mascarenhas

- MOOCs: origins, cMOOC vs xMOOC, accessibility and assessment challenges
- The 21CLD framework: six dimensions, rubrics and decision trees
- Supplementary AI-literacy materials (Rui Ramalho / NotebookLM): LIA, CAP, WISEflow, the AI-supported self-regulation cycle
- **Task 8** – 21CLD decision-tree analysis: apply the ICT-for-learning decision tree (plus one further dimension) to a real classroom activity, identify its rubric level, and submit a PDF with the reasoning

## [[Session 9 — Practical Cases|Session 9 — Practical Cases]]

21 May 2026 · Online (asynchronous), 18:00–20:00 · Daniela Mascarenhas

- Case study: teacher-training project in São Tomé e Príncipe (with World Bank support)
- Case study: the "Paula Frassinetti / CEaD" Moodle-based LMS
- Anchoring earlier frameworks in institutional practice
- No task assigned; delivered as a two-part PDF set

## [[Session 10 — Evaluation and Final Report|Session 10 — Evaluation and Final Report]]

26 May 2026 · Presencial, 18:00–20:00 · Mário Cruz

- Closing session: wrapping up outstanding tasks
- **Final report** – written on the provided template (Modelo do relatório DOCX), submission window 15–26 May 2026
