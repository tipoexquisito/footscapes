# Excalidraw — Diagram Suggestions

A working list of diagrams to create in Excalidraw. Each one has a suggested type, description, and link to the note where it belongs. Create the diagram, export as SVG, and embed with `![[filename.svg]]` at the relevant point in the note.

---

## Priority diagrams

These appear in the most-visited notes and have the highest visual payoff.

DONE ✱1. The Four Zones of Teaching and Learning**
Note: [[The Four Zones of Teaching and Learning]]
Type: 2x2 matrix
Description: Two axes — Challenge (low to high, vertical) and Support (low to high, horizontal). Four quadrants: Learning Zone (top right, highlighted), Frustration Zone (top left), Comfort Zone (bottom right), Boredom Zone (bottom left). Label each zone with a single word and a short descriptor. Use colour to distinguish the Learning Zone from the others.

**2. Vygotsky — Zone of Proximal Development**
Note: [[Vygotsky — Zone of Proximal Development]]
Type: Concentric circles
Description: Three rings. Innermost: "What I can do alone." Middle: "Zone of Proximal Development — what I can do with support." Outer: "Not yet reachable." A small figure or arrow inside the ZPD ring, pointing toward the outer edge. Label the middle ring as the space where scaffolding operates.

**3. The SAMR Model**
Note: [[The SAMR Model]]
Type: Layered ladder or staircase
Description: Four steps ascending left to right — Substitution, Augmentation, Modification, Redefinition. A horizontal dividing line between Augmentation and Modification, labelled "Enhancement / Transformation." Each step has a one-line example. The upper two steps use a warmer or more saturated colour.

**4. Scaffolding — The Build and Remove Logic**
Note: [[Scaffolding]]
Type: Sequence diagram
Description: Three stages side by side. Stage 1: learner + full scaffold (all supports visible). Stage 2: learner + partial scaffold (some supports faded). Stage 3: learner alone (scaffold removed, structure stands). An arrow runs underneath the three stages, left to right, labelled "Competence developing / Support withdrawing."

**5. The Five Pillars of Cooperative Learning**
Note: [[Cooperative Learning]]
Type: Pillar diagram or radial
Description: Five elements arranged as pillars supporting a shared roof — Positive Interdependence, Individual and Group Accountability, Promotive Interaction, Social Skills, Group Processing. The roof is labelled "Cooperative Learning." Alternatively: a radial diagram with "Cooperative Learning" at the centre and the five pillars as spokes.

## Secondary diagrams

These support important notes but are less urgent.

**6. CLIL — The 4 Cs**
Note: [[CLIL]]
Type: Overlapping circles (Venn-style) or grid
Description: Four interlocking elements — Content, Communication, Cognition, Culture. Show how they overlap: the centre (or the strongest overlap) is the CLIL lesson itself. Each circle has a one-sentence descriptor.

**7. Universal Design for Learning — The Three Principles**
Note: [[Universal Design for Learning]]
Type: Triangle or three-column visual
Description: Three columns or three points of a triangle — Multiple Means of Engagement (Why), Multiple Means of Representation (What), Multiple Means of Action or Expression (How). Each column has a sub-note linking to its dedicated page. The base of the triangle (or footer of the columns) reads: "Expert Learner."

**8. AI Literacy — Three Dimensions**
Note: [[AI Literacy]]
Type: Stacked triangle or radial
Description: Three interlocking dimensions — Technical Knowledge, Durable Skills, Future-Ready Attitudes. Each dimension has two or three sub-components listed. A small icon or symbol distinguishes each. A subtle human figure at the centre signals the human-agency framing.

**9. Disseminare — Seeds and Soil**
Note: [[Disseminare]]
Type: Organic / illustrative
Description: A hand releasing seeds (or a scattering motion). Around the hand, different soil types are labelled: institution, colleagues, students, public, digital. Some seeds are mid-air, some have landed. The message: dissemination is not broadcasting — it is scattering into context.

**10. The Learning Pyramid**
Note: [[The Learning Pyramid]]
Type: Pyramid with retention percentages
Description: Classic pyramid divided into horizontal bands, top to bottom: Lecture (5%), Reading (10%), Audio-Visual (20%), Demonstration (30%), Discussion (50%), Practice by Doing (75%), Teaching Others (90%). Colour intensity increases toward the base. A note below the diagram: "Attributed to NTL Institute — retention rates are illustrative, not empirical."

## Embed instructions

Once a diagram is created and exported as SVG from Excalidraw:

1. Save the SVG file in the same folder as the note it belongs to (or in a shared `_assets/` folder).
2. In the note, embed it at the relevant section with `![[diagramname.svg]]`.
3. In Excalidraw, keep the `.excalidraw` source file in `_assets/excalidraw/` so diagrams remain editable.
