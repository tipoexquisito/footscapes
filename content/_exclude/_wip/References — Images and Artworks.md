# References — Images and Artworks

Guidelines for crediting reproductions of artworks and images sourced from the web, museum archives, or Wikimedia Commons.

For a full list of image sources and copyright status for each artwork in the vault, see [[Artwork Images — Suggestions]].

---

## Public domain artworks

Works by artists who died more than 70 years ago are in the public domain in most countries. The reproduction itself may still carry rights if the photograph was taken by a third party — always source from Wikimedia Commons or museum open-access programmes, which explicitly release their reproductions.

**APA format:**
Artist Last Name, First Initial. (Year of work). *Title of work* [Medium]. Museum Name, City. Source URL

Example:
Goya y Lucientes, F. (1814). *El dos de mayo de 1808 en Madrid* [Oil on canvas]. Museo Nacional del Prado, Madrid. https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:El_dos_de_mayo_de_1808_en_Madrid.jpg

**In the note — using a web-hosted image:**

```
![El 2 de mayo de 1808 en Madrid, Francisco de Goya, 1814](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/e/ed/Goya_-_Second_of_May_1808.jpg)
*Francisco de Goya y Lucientes, El 2 de mayo de 1808 en Madrid, 1814. Oil on canvas, 268 × 347 cm. Museo Nacional del Prado, Madrid.*
```

**In the note — using a locally stored image:**

```
![[goya-2-mayo.jpg]]
*Francisco de Goya y Lucientes, El 2 de mayo de 1808 en Madrid, 1814. Oil on canvas, 268 × 347 cm. Museo Nacional del Prado, Madrid.*
```

## Works still under copyright

Picasso (d. 1973) and Dumile Feni (d. 1991) are not yet in the public domain in Spain or most of Europe. Do not embed reproductions without a licence.

**For these works, use a reference link instead:**

```
[Guernica — Museo Reina Sofía](https://www.museoreinasofia.es/coleccion/obra/guernica)
*Pablo Picasso, Guernica, 1937. Oil on canvas, 349 × 776 cm. Museo Nacional Centro de Arte Reina Sofía, Madrid.*
```

## Museum open-access programmes

The following institutions provide high-resolution images free of copyright restrictions for public domain works:

- **Museo Nacional del Prado** — [www.museodelprado.es](https://www.museodelprado.es) (search each work; download button available for open-access files)
- **Museo Nacional Centro de Arte Reina Sofía** — open access for public domain works only
- **Wikimedia Commons** — [commons.wikimedia.org](https://commons.wikimedia.org) — preferred general source; check the licence tag on each file page

---

## Caption standard for this vault

Caption format: *Artist full name, Title in original language, Year. Medium, dimensions. Institution, City.*

Dimensions are optional but useful for context — Las Meninas at 318 × 276 cm and Guernica at 349 × 776 cm are facts that shape how you read the work.
