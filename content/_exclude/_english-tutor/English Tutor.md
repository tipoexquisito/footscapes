# English Tutor
_A reading practice. A writing practice. A thinking practice._

You are not studying English. You are using English to think about things that matter.

Every text in this archive was chosen because it connects to your work as an educator, a designer and a curious human. Some connections are obvious. Others will only reveal themselves later.

Read. Write. Bring it to your tutor. Document what stays.

---

## How it works

Each session follows the same rhythm.

1. Pick the next unread text.
2. Search for it online. Title + author + "free text" or "PDF" or the source publication.
3. Read it. No more than 5 minutes.
4. Write one paragraph in English. No tools. No revision. Raw.
5. Bring the paragraph to your Claude chat for feedback.
6. Copy the feedback into the note. Mark it as reflected.
7. Move to the next level.

## Pairings

Some texts speak directly to each other. Read these together when you are ready.

Rancière + McLuhan. Learner agency meets media ecology.
de Certeau + Carver. Navigation and empathy as design principles.
Lorde + Spivak. Inclusion beyond representation.
Benjamin + Loos. Visual culture, reproduction and design ideology.
Russell + Illich. Two radical critiques of work, attention and institutional learning.
Freire + bell hooks. Critical pedagogy as relational practice.
Barthes + Berger. Who makes meaning: the author or the reader?
Calvino + Haraway. Imagined cities, imagined bodies, imagined futures.
Woolf + Hughes. Voice, identity and the conditions for creative thought.
Whitehead + Sontag. Rhythm, attention and the education of sensibility.
