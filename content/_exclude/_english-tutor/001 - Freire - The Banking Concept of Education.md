## The story behind it

Written in exile after Freire was expelled from Brazil for teaching peasants to read, this text names the violence hidden inside conventional education. The year was 1968. The world was on fire.

Full text: [[Freire - The Banking Concept of Education - Text]]

## Reading prompt

Before you read, hold one question in mind.
**What would it mean to design a learning experience that cannot be deposited?**

After you read, notice:
1. How Freire describes the relationship between narrator and listener.
2. Where power hides in the structure of a lesson.
3. One moment from your own teaching that this text names.

---

## Reflection
_Write one paragraph in English. No revision. No tools. Raw thought.
Bring it to your Claude chat when ready._

```
Designing a learning experience that cannot be deposited, may mean the death of the narrator in education. If narrating means filling the receptacles with phrases deprovided with meaning, critical thinking could never occur. Structuring a lesson that empowers critical thinking must be a mutual agreement. The teacher needs to allow himself to loose authority and the student must take part in defining his own curriculum. In the end, they will be both teachers and learners. I resonate with this text, as I try, in my own practice, to enable opportunities to develop critical thinking moments, even if I struggle to loose control.
```
