Fiat Corde was written for ten minutes on a stage. Not everything that belonged to the thinking behind it belonged to the talk. This note keeps what was cut, as topics rather than prose, so the curation is a record of a choice rather than a loss.

- **Toledo, the city of three cultures.** The original working draft closed the Madrid section with Toledo: synagogue, mosque and cathedral built on top of each other across centuries, without erasing what came before. It was cut for pace, but the argument belongs with [[The City as Classroom]] and could anchor its own reflection or field note.

- **Phenomenon-based learning.** The Finnish model — starting from a real phenomenon and letting disciplines cross through it rather than teaching them in sequence — was named explicitly in an earlier draft as the frame for the Neuromarketing visit to Centro de Arte Oliva. Cut from the talk for length, but it is the clearest existing bridge between [[Context4Content]] and formal curriculum theory, and deserves its own framework note.

- **Velázquez, El Bosco, and the layers excluded by curation.** Fiat Corde.md already records this exclusion explicitly: Velázquez, El Bosco, and the Zapping / RTP / Estado Novo / censorship thread were curated out of the deck and marked as destined for the [[Experience/CAO Visit Project|CAO Visit Project]] note in the Experience section. This entry exists so that note has a paper trail to build from when it is written.

- **The forty hours, unpacked.** The talk compresses "quarenta horas sobre metodologias ativas, aprendizagem cooperativa, CLIL, Universal Design, inteligência artificial na educação" into one line. Each of those threads already has its own framework note in this garden; this is a reminder that the talk is a doorway, not a summary, and the [[Learning/Active Methodologies - Teaching and Learning/Frameworks/index|Frameworks index]] is where the doorway leads.
