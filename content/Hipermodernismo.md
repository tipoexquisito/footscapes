# Hipermodernismo

Term coined by philosopher Gilles Lipovetsky, later developed with Sébastien Charles in *Les temps hypermodernes* (2004). Hypermodernity is not a break with modernity, the way postmodernity claimed to be. It is modernity taken to its extreme: the same commitments to progress, reason and individualism, now unrestrained.

Critic Miguel Real borrows the term to describe the structure of [[Hoje, 3 de Maio]], [[Patrícia Portela]]'s novel built around [[Goya]]'s [[El 3 de mayo]]. See [[O Hipermodernismo - Patrícia Portela, Hoje 3 de Maio]] for the full reading.

Not yet developed further in this garden. A candidate for later use wherever non-linear structure, simultaneity or the refusal of chronology needs a name; [[Notes on Fiat Corde]] and its debt to Portela's non-linearity are the obvious first test.
