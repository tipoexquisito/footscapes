# Hoje, 3 de Maio

[[Patrícia Portela]], 2024.

A novel built entirely from one painting: [[Goya]]'s [[El 3 de mayo]]. Not about brushwork. About mentalities, who composed the firing squad, who was killed, what the street and the aristocracy each remember of that morning in Madrid.

The book offers no single order of reading. One path opens at the page holding the painting, lets you choose a detail, and follows the journey from there. It ends when there is nothing left to discover. Critic Miguel Real reads this structure through [[Hipermodernismo]], time as simultaneity rather than chronology, eight sections that echo one another rather than follow in sequence. See [[O Hipermodernismo - Patrícia Portela, Hoje 3 de Maio]].

> "Viver uma guerra à distância é como olhar para este quadro. É estar lá sem estar dentro, é estar de fora sem estar cá fora."
> — Patrícia Portela, *Hoje, 3 de Maio*

This line, and the book's refusal of chronology, sit behind the non-linear montage of [[Notes on Fiat Corde]].
