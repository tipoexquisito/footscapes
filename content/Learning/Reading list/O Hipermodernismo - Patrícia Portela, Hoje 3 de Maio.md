# O Hipermodernismo: Patrícia Portela, Hoje 3 de Maio

Reading notes on Miguel Real's review, published in Jornal Maio.

Real reads [[Hoje, 3 de Maio]] against Lipovetsky's [[Hipermodernismo]]: a structure built on simultaneity instead of sequence, eight sections that repeat and answer each other rather than advance a plot, from what Real calls Necrose to Germinação. Time here works less like a line and more like a score read from several starting points at once.

He places [[Patrícia Portela]] in a line that runs through José Saramago, who tried something similar: showing several moments of a story at once rather than one after another. Real also brings in Einstein and Bergson on the nature of time, set against the novel's narrator in Madrid.

The review's throughline: the novel is not a description of [[Goya]]'s painting. It is an argument for reading it as several testimonies happening together, the condemned, the firing squad, the street, the aristocracy, none of them prior to the others.
