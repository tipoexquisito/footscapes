# Session 4 — Developing an Online Course: From Needs to Implementation

**Date:** 26 March 2026 · **Format:** Online (asynchronous), 18:00–21:00 · **Facilitator:** Mário Cruz

This session addressed the practical arc of building an online course, from analysing learner needs through to implementation, including the administrative and bureaucratic dimensions that often go unmentioned in design conversations.

Microlearning was introduced as a structuring principle: single-objective, outcome-based, bite-sized units of content that can be digital or analogue. The session also looked at how gamified tasks can sustain engagement among students attending standard daytime schedules, where attention and motivation compete with many other demands. Of all the decks in the programme, this one carried the heaviest media load (over sixty embedded video clips), reinforcing its focus on multimedia content delivery.

> [!note] Task 4 — Narrated video presentation
> Using Microsoft PowerPoint's slide-recording feature (guided by Microsoft's own [tutorial on recording narration and timings](https://support.microsoft.com/pt-pt/office/gravar-uma-apresenta%C3%A7%C3%A3o-de-diapositivos-com-as-temporiza%C3%A7%C3%B5es-de-diapositivos-e-narra%C3%A7%C3%A3o-0b9502c6-5f6c-40ae-b1e7-e47d8741161c)), participants built a short presentation (3–5 slides maximum) on a topic from one of their curricular areas, structured around three moments: introducing the topic and its relevance, explaining a concept with examples or visuals, and proposing a short activity, reflection question, or practical application. The recording had to include narration and slide timings, simulating a resource usable in face-to-face, hybrid, or online teaching.
