# Session 8 — Current and Emerging Trends in e/b-Learning

**Date:** 14 May 2026 · **Format:** Presencial (face-to-face), 18:00–20:00 · **Facilitator:** Daniela Mascarenhas

This session opened with Massive Open Online Courses: their origins in the open educational resources movement of 2008, the distinction between cMOOCs and xMOOCs (Daniel, 2012), and the tension between their promise of scale and accessibility and the practical challenges of assessment and academic integrity.

The bulk of the session was given to the 21st Century Learning Design framework (21CLD, developed through Microsoft's Innovative Teaching and Learning research). Its six dimensions, knowledge construction, collaboration, real-world problem-solving and innovation, skilled communication, self-regulation, and the use of ICT for learning, were examined one by one, with attention to how each defines learner and teacher roles, and how each is mapped through assessment rubrics and decision trees.

> [!note] Task 8 — 21CLD decision-tree analysis
> Starting from the 21CLD model and its six twenty-first-century competencies (knowledge construction, collaboration, real-world problem-solving and innovation, skilled communication, self-regulation, ICT for learning), participants analysed the criteria and decision trees used to assess learning activities in their own subjects:
>
> 1. Choose an activity or task from a subject they teach.
> 2. Apply the decision tree for the ICT-for-learning dimension, plus one further dimension of their choice.
> 3. Identify the activity's level against the 21CLD rubric indicators for both dimensions.
>
> Submission: a PDF presenting and describing the chosen activity, with the reasoned application of both decision trees.

A supplementary set of materials, prepared with NotebookLM and associated with Rui Ramalho (ESEPF), extended this session's theme of trends with a focus on artificial intelligence in education: concepts such as the "Tríade da Experiência Formativa," the AI-supported cycle of self-regulation (plan, monitor, reflect), the "Matriz de Empoderamento na Era da Automação," "distância transacional," institutional tools such as the "LIA" assistant and "Caderno Aberto," the "Cartão de Aprendizagem (CAP)" continuous portfolio model, and the WISEflow assessment platform.
