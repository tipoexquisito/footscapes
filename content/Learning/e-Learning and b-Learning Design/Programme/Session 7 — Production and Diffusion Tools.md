# Session 7 — e/b-learning Kit III & IV: Production and Diffusion Tools

**Date:** 7 May 2026 · **Format:** Presencial (face-to-face), 18:00–20:00 · **Facilitator:** Mário Cruz

The seventh session combined two strands of the original programme, content production and content diffusion, into a single working session.

On the production side, it covered PowerPoint with narration (for flipped-classroom videos) and Genially, used for interactive content, infographics and microsites. On the diffusion side, it introduced Kahoot, Quizizz and Mentimeter as gamified quiz and live-feedback platforms.

TikTok received its own dedicated treatment, supported by detailed facilitator notes that went well beyond the slide bullets. They acknowledged the scepticism many educators feel toward the platform, then walked through concrete classroom uses: using its videos to illustrate curricular topics in the students' own register, building short tip-style clips students can revisit while studying, and asking students to record video explanations of content they would otherwise write down. Practical guidance covered building a teacher profile (naming it clearly as a teaching account, ideally naming the subject taught), developing a recognisable on-camera style, studying what other teachers already do without imitating them, and using relevant education hashtags to help students find the content.

A recurring thread connected these tools to deeper learning aims: building a Kahoot quiz, for instance, was framed as an exercise in research and critical thinking (drawing on Schleicher, 2014) rather than as an end in itself.

> [!note] Task 7 — Educational chatbot with Mizou
> Using [Mizou](https://www.mizou.com), participants designed a personalised educational chatbot and tried it out in a real teaching situation:
>
> 1. **Build it.** Create a chatbot around a curricular theme (a key concept, a problem to solve, a historical-figure simulation, a virtual tutor), and configure its instructions and pedagogical aim.
> 2. **Use it.** Share the chatbot link with students and set a small task around it, clarifying doubts, exploring a problem, simulating a dialogue, or gathering ideas for discussion, either in class or as preparation/consolidation work.
> 3. **Reflect.** Consider the kind of interactions students had with it, its potential to support learning, and its limitations or areas for improvement.
>
> Submission: a Word document with the link to the Mizou chatbot and a short reflection (5–8 lines) on how the activity unfolded with students.
