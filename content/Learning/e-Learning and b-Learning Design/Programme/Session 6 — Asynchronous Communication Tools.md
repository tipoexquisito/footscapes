# Session 6 — e/b-learning Kit II: Asynchronous Communication Tools

**Date:** 30 April 2026 · **Format:** Presencial (face-to-face), 18:00–21:00 · **Facilitator:** Mário Cruz

This session turned to tools that do not depend on simultaneous presence: email, discussion forums, blogs and wikis.

Forums were presented across their different formats, general discussion, question-and-answer, with their subscription and notification mechanisms. Blogs were described as individual, chronologically ordered "log files" that are not inherently collaborative, in contrast to wikis, which support both collective and individual editing, retain version history, and can serve as online books or personal journals in a classroom setting.

The session also looked at how linking and quoting within email threads can build argumentative sequences over time, and at the working culture that asynchronous collaboration requires within a team. The accompanying task then moved this idea of sustained, structured dialogue into a different platform built specifically for it.

> [!note] Task 6 — Structured debate with Kialo Edu
> Using [Kialo Edu](https://www.kialo-edu.com), participants designed and ran a small structured debate in one of their own classes:
>
> 1. **Set it up.** Define a debatable central question or claim tied to a curricular topic, and seed the debate with initial arguments for and against.
> 2. **Run it.** Present the question to students and invite them to add arguments, counter-arguments, or examples, either live or over a defined window after class.
> 3. **Reflect.** Consider the level of student participation, the kind of arguments produced, and the tool's strengths and limits in that subject context.
>
> Submission: a Word document with the link to the Kialo Edu debate and a short reflection (5–8 lines) on how the activity unfolded in class.
