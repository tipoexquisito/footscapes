# Session 2 — e/b-learning: From Virtual Learning Environments to Communities of Practice

**Date:** 12 March 2026 · **Format:** Online (synchronous), 18:00–20:30 · **Facilitator:** Mário Cruz

This session shifted the focus from infrastructure to relationships. It examined how virtual learning environments can develop into communities of practice, built on a sense of belonging that mirrors local communities.

Key ideas included discursive negotiation and the collaborative construction of meaning, and the role of communication tools, email, audio and video conferencing, discussion groups, forums, as instruments that extend cognition rather than simply transmit information. The session framed the choice of a tool as inseparable from the kind of community it is meant to sustain. The deck was unusually media-rich for this edition (over fifty embedded video clips), used to illustrate these ideas in practice rather than only in theory.

> [!note] Task 2 — Virtual Escape Room with Genially
> A two-stage task built around [Genially](https://genial.ly):
>
> 1. **Experience first.** Participants worked through an existing escape room ([*Um sistema aterrador*](https://view.genial.ly/63544ea644e22a0019d69954/interactive-content-um-sistema-aterrador)), paying attention to how the narrative was built, the kind of challenges and puzzles used, how the user progressed, and the format's potential in an educational setting. A short tutorial video, *"Genially: meu primeiro Escape Room — gamificação no ensino,"* supported this stage.
> 2. **Design next.** Participants created a free Genially account, chose a free escape-room template, adapted it into an original activity tied to a curricular topic, and built in challenges or clues to drive progression.
>
> Submission: a Word document with the public link to the created Escape Room and a 150–250 word reflection on its pedagogical potential, covering student motivation, active learning, problem-solving, and collaborative work.
