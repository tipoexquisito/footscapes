# e-/b-Learning Design for Basic and Secondary Education

The classroom did not disappear during the pandemic. It migrated. And for most teachers, that migration was improvised, unplanned, and deeply disorienting.

This teacher training programme starts from that rupture. It does not treat e-/b-learning as a technical upgrade to existing practice. It treats it as a design challenge. The question is not which tool to use, but how to think about learning when the walls become optional.

This edition was delivered at the Centro de Formação Terras de Santa Maria, in 25 contact hours — an adaptation of the original *Conceção de Atividades/UC em E/B-Learning* course (30h), developed at the Centro de Inovação Pedagógica of the Politécnico do Porto by Mário Cruz and Daniela Mascarenhas. The adaptation keeps the conceptual spine of the original while reshaping pace, scope and tasks for a shorter, more concentrated format. Participants move progressively from learner to designer, from consumer of digital tools to architect of digital experience.

## Objectives

The course is structured around ten learning objectives:

A. Reflect on the challenges of distance education in its digital forms: e-learning and b-learning.
B. Know the environments, contexts and platforms of distance education.
C. Mobilise concepts related to e/b-learning in educational practice.
D. Use distance learning platforms, including those available in your institution.
E. Transform a face-to-face course into a distance learning format.
F. Know and use synchronous and asynchronous communication tools.
G. Know and use content production and content diffusion tools.
H. Implement assessment strategies in distance learning modalities.
I. Critically analyse e/b-learning experiences in educational institutions.
J. Design and develop a prototype e-learning or b-learning course for real implementation.

## Methodology

The course unfolds in b-learning mode: face-to-face sessions alternate with online work through an LMS platform. Synchronous sessions use Zoom; asynchronous engagement happens through the platform at each participant's own pace.

The approach centres on collaborative learning drawn from each participant's professional experience. Strategies include practical workshops, case studies, pedagogical experimentation, individual and group tasks, and structured debate. The course does not ask participants to learn tools in isolation. It asks them to situate tools within a pedagogical intent.

Three phases of reflection anchor the process throughout: self-diagnosis at the start, ongoing formative reflection, and a final auto-reflection at the close. The assumption is that teachers who can reflect on their own learning can design better learning for others.

---

## Assessment

Assessment operates across three vectors: diagnostic (at the start), formative (continuous throughout) and summative (at the end).

The final grade is weighted on a scale of 0–20:

- **Course tasks:** 50%
- **Quality participation in synchronous and asynchronous sessions:** 20%
- **Final self-reflection:** 30%
