Venetian painter, 1518–1594, known for his dramatic light, dynamic composition and an approach to biblical narrative that emphasised physical energy and spatial complexity.

[[El Lavatorio]] (The Washing of Feet), c. 1548–1549, in the Prado is a characteristic example: the moment depicted is intimate and spiritual, but the spatial construction is theatrical and expansive, with figures receding into deep perspective and light cutting across the scene with unusual force.
