## What It Is

Guernica is a response to the bombing of the Basque town of Guernica on 26 April 1937, carried out by Nazi German and Fascist Italian forces in support of Franco during the Spanish Civil War.

[[Picasso]] was not present. He built the painting from reports, newspapers, photographs, and indirect testimony. It does not attempt to document the event literally. It constructs emotional and civilisational truth.

---

## The Visual Language

The expressionist distortion is not a stylistic choice — it is the logic of the work. Fragmented bodies, animals in agony, silent screams, spatial collapse: the destruction of form mirrors the destruction of humanity.

The palette is monochrome: grey, black, white. The absence of colour is the absence of life.

---

## The Inheritance of Goya

The line from [[Goya]] is direct and deliberate.

As in [[El 2 de mayo de 1808 en Madrid]] and [[El 3 de mayo en Madrid]], Guernica rejects heroism, refuses epic narrative, and shows war as chaos, mutilation, and dehumanisation. Modern art becomes a form of moral witness.

---

## The Post-Colonial Extension

The work did not remain enclosed within its European context. [[African Guernica]] by [[Dumile Feni]] displaces the narrative — the suffering represented by Picasso becomes a dialogue with colonialism, apartheid, and structural violence across different geographies.

> "History doesn't repeat itself, it does rhyme."
