## What It Shows

The angel Gabriel announces to Mary that she will bear the son of God. The scene is set in a loggia of classical columns and arched vaults; a garden opens to the left. Light enters from multiple sources and is carefully distributed across the surface.

---

## Geometric Certainty as Emotional Register

[[Fra Angelico]]'s composition is built on mathematical precision: the columns establish rhythm, the arches frame each figure, the tile floor creates perspectival depth. The geometry is not cold — it is reassuring. It produces a sense of ordered meaning, of an event that belongs to an intelligible universe.

The light, distributed evenly, makes every surface readable. Nothing is hidden in shadow.

---

## Multimodality and Visual Design

The painting is a study in **how visual design communicates before content does**: proportion, rhythm, colour, and spatial organisation all carry meaning independently of the depicted scene.

This connects to principles of instructional design: the structure of a learning environment (its visual logic, spatial arrangement, and pacing) shapes the learner's experience before a word is read.
