Pablo Ruiz Picasso (1881–1973). Spanish painter and sculptor, co-founder of Cubism. In this garden, he appears through two lenses: the artist as witness, and the artist as someone who never stopped being a child.

> "Every child is an artist. The problem is how to remain an artist once we grow up."

---

## Picasso as War Reporter

Picasso did not witness the bombing of Guernica directly. He built [[Guernica]] from reports, newspapers, photographs, and indirect testimony. Yet the painting does not try to document the event literally. It constructs emotional and civilisational truth.

The expressionist distortion — fragmented bodies, animals in agony, silent screams, broken spatial logic — translates the collapse of humanity itself. In this, Picasso stands alongside [[Goya]]: both refuse the epic narrative of war, both make violence visible by refusing to make it beautiful.

---

## What "Remaining an Artist" Means

The quote became one of the recurring anchors of the week. To remain an artist is to remain:

- curious
- experimental
- playful
- open to interpretation
- sensitive to what is in front of you

It is the opposite of rigidity. It connects directly to [[Notes on Unlearning]] — the course's implicit argument that good teaching requires unlearning the assumption that we already know how things should be done.
