Miguel de Cervantes Saavedra (1547–1616). Spanish writer, author of *Don Quixote de la Mancha* (1605/1615), considered the first modern novel.

> "La pluma es la lengua del alma."
> "The quill is the tongue of the soul."

---

## Narrative Modernity

*Don Quixote* is considered the first modern novel not because of its form but because its characters evolve. They are not flat. They carry interior transformation, ambiguity, and complexity — qualities that distinguish a character who changes through experience from one who merely enacts a role.

Genuine learning requires the same: transformation, ambiguity, evolution, and the willingness to hold complexity without resolving it prematurely.

Don Quixote is a figure who learns against the resistance of the world. Sancho Panza learns through proximity and concrete experience. Both are models of learning — different kinds, equally real.

## Connection to the Course

Cervantes appeared as a cultural reference across Toledo and Madrid. The phrase *La pluma es la lengua del alma* framed the practice of reflective writing and the [[Learning Portfolios]] — the idea that writing is not a record of thought but a mode of thought itself.
