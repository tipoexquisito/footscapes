Francisco de Goya y Lucientes (1746–1828). Spanish painter, court artist to Carlos IV, and one of the sharpest political witnesses in the history of Western art.

---

## Goya as War Reporter

Goya anticipated modern photojournalism — not by documenting events literally, but by capturing their emotional and political truth. He did not seek the heroic. He sought the human.

His work does not glorify. It witnesses, denounces, humanises victims, and documents violence as a system rather than an incident. In this, he stands alongside [[Picasso]] as a forerunner of the war correspondent — a recorder of what power does to bodies.
