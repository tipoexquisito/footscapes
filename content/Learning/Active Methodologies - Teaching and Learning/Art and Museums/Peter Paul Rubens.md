Flemish Baroque painter, 1577–1640, and one of the most prolific and influential artists of the seventeenth century. Rubens worked across genre, scale and medium with remarkable facility, and his studio produced works for clients across Europe.

The Prado holds several major works. [[Las tres Gracias]] (c. 1635) is among his most celebrated — a painting of abundance, sensory pleasure and classical reference that also carries his personal mark: one of the three figures bears the features of his young wife.

[[The Judgement of Paris]] presents the mythological contest with characteristic Baroque energy, the goddesses rendered with the same physical presence as the world around them.
