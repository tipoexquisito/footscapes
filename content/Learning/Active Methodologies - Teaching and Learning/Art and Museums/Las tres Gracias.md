## What It Shows

Three female figures — the Three Graces of classical mythology — in a circular embrace. The composition turns on itself: no figure is frontal, each connects to the next, the group forms a closed loop.

---

## The Compositional Logic

The circularity is the work's central argument. Harmony is not hierarchy; it is reciprocity. Each figure supports and is supported by the others. The garland they hold together is an extension of that logic.

[[Peter Paul Rubens|Rubens]]' characteristic luminous flesh and dynamic line give the figures weight and movement simultaneously — presence without stasis.

---

## Connections in the Course

The circular composition connects to cooperative learning structures: interdependence, mutual support, no single figure dominating. The group as something greater than the sum of its parts.

See [[Cooperative Learning]], [[Positive Interdependence]], and the pedagogical value of structures that produce **relational learning**.
