# Museums & Visual Culture

Art was not decoration in this course. It was methodology.

The paintings appeared before they were named. A quiz on the first evening asked about [[Goya]] — not as art history, but as a war reporter. It was [[El 3 de mayo en Madrid]], in Madrid, the city where the painting was made. That coincidence was not accidental. The coordinator confirmed it on Thursday morning: the programme had always been built this way.

By the end of the week, [[Las Meninas]] had appeared in a quiz, a [[Padlet]] activity, a Prado visit, a [[Portfolio Development]] mind map and a slide deck. Not as an illustration of visual literacy. As a structure for thinking about who is watching, who is watched, and what is happening outside the frame. In a classroom, those questions are never purely aesthetic.

This section collects the works, the artists and the cultural spaces that became part of the week's argument.

---

## Museo del Prado

The guided visit on Wednesday afternoon — [[Highlights of Spanish Art]] — moved through fourteen works across six centuries. The sequence was curatorial, not chronological: a slow argument about how images construct meaning.

**Artists and works:**

- [[Fra Angelico]]: certainty, gold, the sacred as legible — the week's starting point
- [[Rogier van der Weyden]] — [[El Descendimiento]]: grief made sculptural, emotion as compositional force
- [[Hieronymus Bosch]] — [[Tríptico del Jardín de las delicias]]: a world without stable ground, meaning that refuses to resolve
- [[El Greco]] — [[El caballero de la mano en el pecho]], [[La Anunciación]], [[La Crucifixión]], [[El entierro del conde de Orgaz]]: the painter who arrived from elsewhere and defined the place
- [[Jacopo Tintoretto]] — [[El Lavatorio]]: perspective as power, who kneels and who does not
- [[Peter Paul Rubens]] — [[Las tres Gracias]], [[The Judgement of Paris]]: the body as argument, cooperation as circular form
- [[Diego Velázquez]] — [[Felipe IV]], [[Las Meninas]]: the question that ends the visit and opens everything else
- [[Goya]] — [[El 2 de mayo de 1808 en Madrid]], [[El 3 de mayo en Madrid]], [[La familia de Carlos IV]]: witness, irony, the refusal to glorify

## Museo Nacional Centro de Arte Reina Sofía

Saturday afternoon. Self-guided, with an audioguide as scaffold. Four to five hours after the course had officially ended.

- [[Museo Nacional Centro de Arte Reina Sofia]]: the afternoon in full
- [[Avant-Garde Territories]]: the ruptures that made the twentieth century visible
- [[Guernica]]: the room the course had been building toward since Sunday evening
- [[African Guernica]]: [[Dumile Feni]], 1967 — what the image looks like from elsewhere
- [[Arte contemporânea pós-1975]]: what came after

## Toledo

[[El Greco]] spent most of his working life in Toledo. The visit to his city — and to the church where [[El entierro del conde de Orgaz]] hangs in a darkened room — completed a thread that had run since Wednesday.

---

## Literary & Cultural Figures

The week's reference points were not only visual.

- [[Cervantes]] — *"La pluma es la lengua del alma."* Writing as the externalisation of thought.
- [[Don Quixote]] and [[Sancho Panza]]: the idealist and the pragmatist, the two figures every educator navigates between.
- [[Picasso]] — *"Every child is born an artist. The problem is how to remain an artist once we grow up."*

## Key Themes

- [[Curation]]: the act of selecting, sequencing and contextualising as pedagogical design
- [[Visual Learning]]: not using images, but developing the capacity to read them
- [[Place-based Learning]]: the museum as learning environment, not destination
- [[Multisensory Learning]]: how embodied encounter with art differs from description
- [[Context4Content]]: culture at both registers — the canonical and the everyday
