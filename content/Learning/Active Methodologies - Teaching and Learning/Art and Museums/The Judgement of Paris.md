## What It Shows

The myth of Paris, prince of Troy, choosing the most beautiful among three goddesses: Juno, Minerva and Venus. Paris awards Venus the golden apple. The choice that sets the Trojan War in motion.

---

## Subjectivity as Subject

The painting's core problem is that Paris must judge — and any judgement of this kind is irreducibly subjective, partial, and consequential.

[[Peter Paul Rubens|Rubens]] does not adjudicate. He shows the moment of decision itself: three figures presented for evaluation, the judge looking, the choice not yet made. The painting holds the viewer inside that moment of interpretive responsibility.

---

## Connections in the Course

The myth connects to **critical thinking**: the capacity to evaluate competing options without assuming any single criterion is universal or neutral. Who sets the standard? Whose definition of value counts?

This links to [[Healthy scepticism]], [[AI Literacy]] (training data reflects whose judgements?), and the broader theme of interpretation as an active, positioned act.
