## What It Is

A triptych in three panels: Eden on the left, an imagined world of earthly pleasure at the centre, Hell on the right. The work is painted at a scale that demands slow navigation. There is no correct reading sequence, no single interpretive line.

---

## The Pedagogical Explosive

The Garden of Earthly Delights is what inquiry-based learning looks like as an object.

Every centimetre of the surface contains something unexpected. A fish carrying a human. A bird the size of a building. Groups engaged in activity that resists categorisation. The eye moves not toward resolution but toward further questions.

It does not explain itself. It invites — and demands — extended, active attention.

---

## Non-Linear Visual Hypertext

The triptych operates as a non-linear structure: you enter anywhere, follow threads, return, discover something missed. The experience is closer to hypertext navigation than to linear reading.

This makes it an unusually productive model for thinking about **inquiry-based learning** and **place-based learning**: knowledge as a territory to be explored rather than a sequence to be followed.
