South African artist, 1939–1991, born in the Eastern Cape and later exiled from apartheid South Africa. Feni worked primarily in drawing and printmaking, developing a figurative language of extreme expressive power to document human suffering under apartheid.

[[African Guernica]] (1967) is his most internationally known work, produced in exile in London. The title is not metaphorical — Feni understood Picasso's [[Guernica]] as a direct precursor and positioned his own response to South African violence within that tradition of bearing witness through art.

Feni's work is part of the permanent collection of the Reina Sofía, where it appears in dialogue with Picasso's work and the museum's broader commitment to art as political testimony.

See [[African Guernica]], [[Guernica]], [[Museo Nacional Centro de Arte Reina Sofia]].
