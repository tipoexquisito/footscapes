Thematic itinerary at the [[Museo Nacional Centro de Arte Reina Sofia]]. A cultural archaeology of artistic modernity from the late nineteenth century to the first half of the twentieth.

---

## What the Itinerary Constructs

The itinerary does not present a succession of modernist masterpieces. It builds an understanding of why modernism happened — not as aesthetic evolution but as a response to the radical transformation of human experience.

The avant-gardes did not emerge from art schools. They emerged from the modern city: its acceleration, its industrial noise, its masses, its advertising, its printed culture, its mechanised warfare, its international circulation of ideas. Each movement is legible only inside this ecosystem.

---

## The City as Perceptual Laboratory

Urban modernity fragmented perception. The street became simultaneous, saturated, fast, discontinuous. Cubism is not a formal innovation — it translates a new perceptual condition: multiple viewpoints, simultaneity, the collapse of the fixed observer.

Classical perspective assumed stability, centrality, rational order. Modernity destroyed that stability. Art registered the destruction.

---

## Magazines, Posters, and the End of High Culture

One of the strongest dimensions of the itinerary is its refusal to separate fine art from graphic culture, design, advertising, typography, and mass communication.

Avant-garde magazines functioned simultaneously as manifestos, international networks, curatorial spaces, visual laboratories, and political platforms. Modernism spread not through museums but through printed pages, covers, layouts, posters, and technical reproduction.

This brings the historical avant-gardes surprisingly close to contemporary digital culture: rapid circulation, remix, visual identity, transnational community. The medium changes; the logic of cultural diffusion does not.

---

## The Dissolution of Disciplinary Boundaries

The itinerary shows art, architecture, fashion, set design, urban planning, and politics converging — the modern attempt to make art a total intervention in human experience, not a contemplative object.

The ambivalence is left in the room: the same modernism that promised emancipation also opened the door to massification, anonymity, and technocracy.

---

## Modernity and Childhood

Running through the itinerary: a tension between rationalisation and imagination. The avant-gardes repeatedly attempted to recover spontaneity, play, experiment, and undisciplined language.

This connects directly to [[Picasso]]:
> "Every child is an artist. The problem is how to remain an artist once we grow up."

Avant-garde modernity is, among other things, an attempt to unlearn perceptual convention. See [[Notes on Unlearning]].
