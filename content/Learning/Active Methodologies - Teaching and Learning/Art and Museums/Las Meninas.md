## What It Is

[[Diego Velázquez]] paints himself painting. At the centre of the composition stands the young Infanta Margarita, surrounded by her ladies-in-waiting (the *meninas*). In the background, a mirror reflects the King and Queen — the subjects of the portrait Velázquez is in the process of making. The viewer occupies the position of the royal couple.

The result: a painting about who is looking, who is being looked at, and who controls the frame.

---

## The Question It Asks

Las Meninas does not resolve the question of its own representation. It suspends it.

Who is the subject of the painting? The Infanta? The royal couple reflected in the mirror? The painter himself? The viewer standing where the sitters would have stood?

The work is a sustained interrogation of observation, framing, and power. It refuses to assign a single stable point of view.

---

## Connections in the Course

The painting connects directly to **media literacy**: the idea that every image has a frame, every frame has an author, and every author has a position. What we see is always constructed by someone. See [[What is truth in 2026?]] and [[Healthy scepticism]].

The question of the invisible observer — who controls what is represented — runs through [[AI Literacy]], [[Arte contemporânea pós-1975]], and the week's recurring discussion of representation and power.

Velázquez's self-insertion into the frame also connects to **reflective practice**: the practitioner visible within their own work, not pretending to invisible objectivity.
