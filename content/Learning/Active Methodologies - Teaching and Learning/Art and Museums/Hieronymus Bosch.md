Netherlandish painter, c. 1450–1516, known for his complex, visionary imagery combining theological allegory, folk tradition, satire and what reads, to modern eyes, as surrealism five centuries before the movement.

The Spanish royal collection acquired Bosch's work early, and the Prado holds the largest and most significant collection outside the Netherlands. [[Tríptico del Jardín de las delicias]] (The Garden of Earthly Delights), c. 1490–1510, is his most celebrated work and one of the most analysed paintings in European art. Three panels move from paradise to sin to hell, populated by figures and creatures at a scale that rewards hours of looking.

Bosch's work has no obvious modern parallel. Its closest contemporary relatives are graphic novels, video games and fever dreams.
