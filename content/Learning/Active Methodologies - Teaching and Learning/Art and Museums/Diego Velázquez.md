Court painter to King Philip IV of Spain from 1623 until his death in 1660. Velázquez is one of the most technically accomplished painters in European history, and one of the most conceptually ambitious. His paintings raise questions about representation, illusion, power and the act of looking that have not been exhausted in four centuries.

[[Las Meninas]] (1656) is the most studied painting in the Prado collection and possibly the most discussed painting in Western art. It depicts the royal family, court attendants, a dog — and the viewer, positioned where the king and queen must be standing. The mirror at the back of the room confirms their presence. What appears to be a scene becomes a meditation on what painting does.

[[Felipe IV]] appears in multiple Velázquez portraits in the Prado — a monarch rendered with increasing psychological complexity over the decades of their working relationship.
