## What It Shows

The night after the uprising. French soldiers execute Madrid civilians by firing squad. [[Goya]] painted this six years after the event, alongside the companion work [[El 2 de mayo de 1808 en Madrid]].

---

## The Logic of the Work

There is no heroism here. No glorification. No narrative arc that converts death into sacrifice.

The man in white at the centre raises his arms — but this is not triumph. It is exposure. The gesture is surrender and defiance at once, and it changes nothing. The soldiers are faceless, their backs turned, their rifles aimed with mechanical precision. On one side, individuals; on the other, a system.

Goya strips war down to what it is: the organised killing of people.

---

## The Line to Guernica

The visual language runs directly forward to [[Guernica]]. Monochrome palette, fragmented bodies, the refusal to aestheticise violence. [[Picasso]] knew this painting. The moral inheritance is explicit.
