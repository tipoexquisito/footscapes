## What It Shows

Christ washing the feet of his disciples at the Last Supper. The scene is set in an architectural space of striking depth — columns, arched ceiling, figures receding into a diagonal that drives the eye from the foreground to a distant vanishing point.

---

## The Diagonal as Experience Design

[[Jacopo Tintoretto|Tintoretto]] breaks with the centred, symmetrical composition that conventional religious painting used to signal hierarchy and solemnity.

The diagonal pulls the viewer through the scene. The eye does not rest; it moves. The architectural depth creates a sense of space extending beyond the frame. The experience is immersive rather than contemplative.

This is a deliberate choice about how a viewer encounters the work — and it is a design decision with clear pedagogical resonance: the structure of an experience shapes the quality of the learning.

---

## Connections in the Course

The painting connects to **learning experience design**: the idea that how learning is structured — its flow, sequence, spatial logic, and emotional pacing — determines what learners take away.

The servant-leadership act (Christ washing feet) also connects to the week's discussions of role, power, and the dignity of care. See [[Cooperative Learning]] and [[Promotive Interaction]].
