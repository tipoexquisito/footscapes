Greek-born painter who worked primarily in Toledo, Spain, from the 1570s until his death in 1614. His work is characterised by elongated figures, dramatic light, intense colour and spiritual intensity. El Greco was largely overlooked after his death and was rediscovered in the late nineteenth and early twentieth centuries, when modernist artists recognised in his distortions and expressiveness a precursor to their own experiments.

In Toledo, El Greco is a constant presence. [[El entierro del conde de Orgaz]] in the church of Santo Tomé is perhaps the most significant single work in the city — a painting that compresses earth and heaven, community and eternity, into a single image. [[El caballero de la mano en el pecho]] in the Prado is one of the most psychologically direct portraits of the Spanish Renaissance.
