# Portfolio Development

## First Draft — Tuesday, 6 May 2026

Tuesday lunchtime. No agenda, no structure yet.

The first drafts happened between sessions — over lunch, on phones, on paper. The assignment was to document the week's learning in portfolio form. What came out were fragments: keywords, sketches, frameworks half-remembered from the morning, questions without answers.

![[040-20260505-filipe-draft-presentation-lunch.png]]
*Draft presentation, lunch | Day 3 © Filipe Gama*

![[041-20260505-group-team-first-drafts-during-lunch.png]]
*Team first drafts during lunch | Day 3 © Team*

The question at this stage was not "what do we present?" but "what did we actually do this week?"

## Mind Map — Thursday, 7 May 2026

Thursday afternoon. The two groups merged.

Eight participants had spent the week in two separate groups — two Portuguese, two German and four Italian participants — parallel in programme but not in experience. At lunch on Thursday they decided that presenting two portfolios made no sense. The afternoon became a single collaborative act of synthesis.

![[072-20260507-group-presentation-draft-team-work.png]]
*Presentation draft, team work | Day 5 © Team*

The format became a mind map. Central node: "Innovative and Active Methodologies for Teaching and Learning." Radiating outward: Perspective, Frameworks, Teacher, Content and Context, Time and Space, Communication, Cooperation, The Bigger Picture.

![[073-20260507-group-team-work-presentation-afternoon.png]]
*Filipe (PT), Sílvia (IT), Mónica (PT), Julia (DE) and Maria Grazia (IT) brainstorming | Day 5 © Team*

When the question arose of what to anchor the visual argument, the artworks offered themselves. [[Las Meninas]] for perspective. [[El 3 de mayo en Madrid]] for context and time. [[Las tres Gracias]] for cooperation — three figures in relation, a circular composition with no fixed beginning or end.

![[Perspective presentation.png]]
*A mind map of metaphors and visual anchors*

## Refinements — Friday, 8 May 2026

Friday evening. The team separated to refine independently.

The structure established on Thursday remained intact. What changed was the precision: the language of each node tightened, the slide transitions clarified, the opening activity finalised. The presentation stayed anchored to the same three paintings. Finished in the early hours of Saturday morning.
