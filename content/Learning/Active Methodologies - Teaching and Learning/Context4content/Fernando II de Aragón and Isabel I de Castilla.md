The Catholic Monarchs of Spain, ruling jointly from 1474 (Castile) and 1479 (Aragon).

---

Their union created the political entity that would become Spain. Their reign produced the completion of the *Reconquista* (1492), the expulsion of the Jews (1492), the expulsion of the Moors (1502), the sponsorship of Columbus's voyages, and the foundation of the Spanish Inquisition.

The same year — 1492 — that marks the arrival of Spanish power in the Americas also marks the forced departure of communities who had shaped [[Toledo — The City of Three Cultures]] for centuries.

Power and cultural loss arrived together.
