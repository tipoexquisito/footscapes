# Context4Content

*Context precedes content. Experience precedes explanation. Observation precedes abstraction.*

Context4Content is not one methodology among others. It is the frame that made the whole week coherent.

Its premise: learning does not need to be applied to reality. It is constructed from reality. The city, the museum, the meal, the market, the school corridor, the painting on the wall of a bar — none of these were illustrations of content. They were the content. The frameworks that were named in the classroom — [[Place-based Learning]], [[Situated Learning]], [[Experiential Learning]], [[Multisensory Learning]] — were not being prepared for eventual application. They were already in operation in the street outside.

The coordinator confirmed it on Thursday morning: the programme had always been built this way.

---

## The Framework

The full methodological description lives in [[Context4Content]] (Frameworks). What follows here is the lived version — the section of the garden where Context4Content is not described but demonstrated.

The framework operates through a cycle: immersion, observation, interpretation, articulation, reflection. Each pass through the cycle deepens both the understanding of place and the understanding of pedagogy. The educator's role shifts from content deliverer to [[Notes on Curating Learning|learning curator]]. The learner shifts from receiver to active reader of context.

## The City as Curriculum

### Madrid

Madrid entered the programme before the programme began. [[El Rastro]] on Sunday morning — before any session, before any framework had been named — was already a field project. The market, the _pan con tomate_, the streets of La Latina: an ethnographic encounter with no task card.

Monday afternoon formalised it. [[Images of Madrid]] was a walking tour structured as a quiz across eight locations: Plaza Mayor, Restaurante Botín, the 2 de Mayo plaque, the Royal Palace, Puerta del Sol, Km0. Participants documented through [[Seesaw]], navigated through [[Genially]]. The tour was designed to be non-linear: different routes, different experiences, the same city differently read.

Monday evening offered something no one had planned. [[Sala Equis]] — a cinema bar in Madrid, discovered by the group — had [[Metropolis]] projected on the wall above the bar. Silent. The central line of the film on loop: *The mediator between the head and the hands must be the heart.* Nobody had organised it. Nobody could have.

Tuesday afternoon was listed in the programme as autonomous portfolio work. What it became was [[Casa de Campo]]. A park. Space, movement, deceleration. No task, no output. The morning's frameworks continued, quietly, at a different pace.

[[Gastronomy notes — Madrid as Memory, Ritual and Learning|Gastronomy]] ran across the entire week as its own thread. Bocadillos de calamares as bonus points. Cañas after the walking tour. Churros and hot chocolate at San Ginés. Restaurante Botín. The ritual of eating as a pedagogical act: food as identity system, sensory learning through taste and memory, the discipline of simplicity that produces something more nourishing than elaboration.

### Toledo

Friday afternoon. The city as palimpsest.

[[Toledo — The City of Three Cultures]] holds nine centuries of layered civilisation in its streets. Christian, Jewish, and Moorish traditions in the same buildings, the same distances, the same silences between them. The coexistence was neither frictionless nor equal — it was contested, negotiated, and eventually ended by force. But it produced a city that is architecturally and culturally plural in ways that remain visible today.

Toledo explained Context4Content without using the term.

The marzipan tasted in a morning classroom session was recognised again in an actual shop window. [[Marzipan - The Sweet Bread of Toledo|The marzipan]] is a recipe as cultural history: almonds, sugar, a convent, an absent ingredient, a gesture of survival. Its origin story is inseparable from the expulsions of 1492 — the same year [[Fernando II de Aragón and Isabel I de Castilla]] completed the *Reconquista*, expelled the Jewish and Moorish communities, and sponsored Columbus. Power and cultural loss arrived together in the same calendar year.

[[El Greco]] spent most of his working life in Toledo. The visit to the church where [[El entierro del conde de Orgaz]] hangs in a darkened room completed a thread that had run since Wednesday at the Prado.

## The Museums

[[Highlights of Spanish Art]] at the Museo del Prado on Wednesday afternoon was Context4Content as curatorial argument. Fourteen works across six centuries, sequenced not chronologically but thematically: a slow argument about representation, perspective and the act of looking. The meeting point was the statue of [[Goya]] at the entrance — which was itself the first work of art.

By the end of the week, [[Las Meninas]] had appeared in a quiz, a [[Padlet]] activity, the Prado visit, a portfolio mind map and a slide deck. Not as an illustration of visual literacy. As a structure for thinking about who is watching, who is watched, and what happens outside the frame.

[[Museo Nacional Centro de Arte Reina Sofia]] on Saturday was self-guided, with an audioguide as scaffold. The course had officially ended at midday. Four to five hours followed across all floors: Avant-Garde Territories, [[Guernica]], the [[African Guernica]] by [[Dumile Feni]], Contemporary Art post-1975. Context4Content, after the end.

## The School

[[IES Príncipe Felipe]] on Thursday morning took the frameworks into an institutional field site. Classrooms, laboratories and corridors as objects of study. UDL visible in the spatial layout. CLIL in the multilingual encounter with the school director. Cooperative learning in the student wifi programme. The frameworks made architectural.
