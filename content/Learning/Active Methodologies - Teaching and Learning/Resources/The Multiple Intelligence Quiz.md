A self-assessment tool based on [[Howard Gardner — Multiple Intelligences]]'s framework. Eight categories, each assessed through a set of preference and behaviour statements.

---

## The Eight Categories

**Verbal-Linguistic** — *I enjoy reading, writing, storytelling, and word games. Language is my primary medium.*

**Logical-Mathematical** — *I think in patterns and systems. I enjoy puzzles, reasoning, and working with numbers or abstract structures.*

**Visual-Spatial** — *I think in images. I am good with maps, diagrams, design, and remembering where things are.*

**Interpersonal** — *I understand people easily. I work well in groups, read emotions accurately, and learn from social interaction.*

**Musical-Rhythmic** — *I am sensitive to pitch, rhythm, and melody. Music helps me think or focus.*

**Naturalistic** — *I notice patterns in the natural world. I am drawn to animals, plants, ecology, and outdoor environments.*

**Bodily-Kinesthetic** — *I learn by doing. I process information through movement, touch, and physical experience.*

**Intrapersonal** — *I understand my own thinking and feelings. I prefer working independently and need time for reflection.*

---

## How to Use It

Participants complete the statements, tally their scores per category, and identify their strongest profiles. The debrief question is more important than the results: *What does this tell you about how you learn — and how you teach?*

The quiz is an entry point for discussion, not a diagnostic tool. It opens reflection on learning preferences, teaching style, and the assumptions embedded in classroom design.
