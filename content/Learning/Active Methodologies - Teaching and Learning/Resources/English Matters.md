The organisation has been running since 1992, established formally in 1999, and has built partnerships with universities across Spain, Ireland, Iceland, Finland, Sweden, Norway, Portugal and Estonia. Its programmes span CLIL, active methodologies, AI and digital competences, wellbeing and mindfulness, outdoor and context-based learning, and several levels of English for teachers. The network is wide. But the identity it chooses is not institutional. It is relational.
