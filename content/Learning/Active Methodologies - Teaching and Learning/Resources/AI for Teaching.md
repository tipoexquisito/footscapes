A station rotation session exploring AI tools for educational use. Participants moved through digital stations, each focused on a different tool and application.

---

## The Stations

**Mentimeter** — real-time audience engagement and polling. Live word clouds, Q&A, and quiz formats. Used for formative assessment and activating prior knowledge.

**NotebookLM** — Google's AI-powered research assistant. Ingests documents and generates summaries, questions, and study guides from uploaded materials.

**Curipod** — AI-assisted slide and lesson generator. Creates interactive lessons from a prompt, including discussion questions and activities.

**Padlet Arcade** — collaborative digital wall with gamified elements. Used for brainstorming, sharing resources, and structured group responses.

**WebWise** — digital literacy and online safety resource, particularly focused on younger learners and vulnerable populations.

**Genially** — interactive content creation: presentations, infographics, escape rooms, and multimedia learning objects.

**Canva** — graphic design platform with strong education features including AI image generation and presentation tools.

**Book Creator** — multimodal digital book creation. Learners produce books combining text, image, audio, and video.

---

## The Station Rotation Model

The session used a station rotation structure: groups rotated through each tool in timed intervals, with a facilitator at each station to support exploration and provoke reflection.

The model itself is a demonstration of [[Active Methodologies]] and [[The SAMR Model]]: the technology is not supplementary — it restructures the learning environment.
