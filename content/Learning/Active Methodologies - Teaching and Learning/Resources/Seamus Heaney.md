1939–2013. Irish poet. Born in County Derry, Northern Ireland, into a Catholic farming family. Educated at Queen's University Belfast. Later taught at Harvard and Oxford. Nobel Prize in Literature, 1995.

---

Heaney's poetry is grounded in the physical world — soil, tools, weather, labour — and attentive to the weight of history in ordinary places. His work carries the landscape of rural Ireland, the divisions of the North, and the inheritance of a literary tradition he engaged with as both heir and critic.

He received the Nobel Prize "for works of lyrical beauty and ethical depth, which exalt everyday miracles and the living past."

---

The poem [[Scaffolding (Seamus Heaney)]] uses the image of a builder's scaffold as a metaphor for the provisional structures that make lasting things possible.
