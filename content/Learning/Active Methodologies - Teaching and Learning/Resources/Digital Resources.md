From the [[DigCompEdu]] framework, Area 2: Digital Resources. Three competency areas for the selection, creation, and management of digital educational materials.

---

## Selecting Digital Resources

Identifying, evaluating, and selecting appropriate digital tools, resources, and content for educational purposes.

The key skill here is critical evaluation: not all digital resources are pedagogically sound, accurately evidenced, or contextually appropriate. Selecting well requires knowing what to look for — and what to reject.

---

## Creating and Modifying Digital Resources

Adapting existing digital content or creating new materials suited to the specific learning context, learner group, and pedagogical goals.

This shifts the teacher from consumer to author — a significant professional and creative move. The resource is no longer generic; it is designed for this group, this moment, this purpose.

---

## Managing, Protecting, and Sharing Digital Resources

Organising digital materials effectively, ensuring compliance with copyright and data protection regulations, and sharing resources within appropriate communities of practice.

The sharing dimension connects to : what teachers make, they can give. Professional culture is built from what practitioners make available to each other.
