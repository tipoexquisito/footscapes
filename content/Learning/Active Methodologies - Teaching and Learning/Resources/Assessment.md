From the [[DigCompEdu]] framework, Area 4: Assessment. Three competency areas for using digital tools to support and improve assessment practice.

---

## Assessment Strategies

Using digital technologies to diversify and enrich assessment approaches — moving beyond written tests toward multimodal, collaborative, and formative assessment designs.

Digital tools make it possible to assess process, not only product: to capture drafts, iterations, reflections, and collaborative contributions that a final test cannot reach.

---

## Analysing Evidence

Using digital tools to collect, analyse, and interpret evidence of learning — from learning analytics platforms to simple response-gathering tools like Mentimeter or Padlet.

The key shift: assessment as an ongoing, data-informed practice rather than a periodic event. Formative assessment in real time.

---

## Feedback and Planning

Using digital tools to provide timely, personalised, and actionable feedback — and to use assessment data to inform future planning.

The move from evaluation (judgement at the end) to feedback (guidance during) is one of the most consequential shifts in contemporary pedagogy. Digital tools make scalable personalised feedback more achievable.
