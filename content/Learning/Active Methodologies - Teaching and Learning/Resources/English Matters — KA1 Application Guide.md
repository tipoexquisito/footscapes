Strategic notes on the Erasmus+ KA1 application process, based on the English Matters programme documentation and the experience of applying for this course.

---

## The Institutional Logic

A KA1 application is not a travel request. It is a professional development proposal that must demonstrate institutional need, pedagogical rationale, and planned impact.

The language of the application is significant: Erasmus+ frames mobility not as individual enrichment but as organisational improvement. The institution applies; the individual participates.

---

## The Core Formula

A strong KA1 application follows a coherent argument across six elements:

**Problem** — what is the specific gap or need in the institution's current practice? Not a general aspiration. A concrete, evidenced challenge.

**Objective** — what specific change in knowledge, practice, or attitude will the mobility produce?

**Mobility** — why this course, with this provider, in this location? What makes this the right intervention for this problem?

**Implementation** — how will learning be embedded in institutional practice after the mobility? Who will be involved? What will change?

**Dissemination** — how will the learning reach beyond the individual participant? What channels, what audiences, what timeline?

**Impact** — how will the institution know that the mobility produced the intended change? What indicators? What timeframe?

---

## Mobility Is Not Travel

The distinction the programme emphasises consistently: mobility is a professional development intervention with institutional consequences. It is not a cultural visit with a learning component attached.

This distinction shapes every element of the application — and every decision about what to disseminate and how.
