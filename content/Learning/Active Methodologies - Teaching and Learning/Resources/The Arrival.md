## What It Is

A wordless graphic novel following a man who emigrates to an imagined foreign country — a place with an invented script, unfamiliar fauna, surreal architecture, and social conventions he cannot read. The experience of arrival is rendered without text: the reader inhabits the same disorientation the protagonist feels.

Because the language is invented, no reader is a native speaker. Everyone is equally at a loss.

---

## The Pedagogical Argument

*The Arrival* is one of the most effective tools available for developing empathy toward the experience of linguistic and cultural displacement.

A learner who has never been a migrant, never felt the specific humiliation of not understanding a sign, a gesture, or a social norm — that learner experiences, in twenty minutes of careful reading, a version of what millions of students carry into classrooms every day.

---

## Multimodal and Wordless

The absence of text is not a simplification — it is the point. Meaning is carried entirely through image, composition, facial expression, and visual narrative. This makes the book accessible across language levels and simultaneously demonstrates the expressive power of visual literacy.

It also makes it ideal for [[CLIL]] contexts: the content is rich; the language barrier is removed.
