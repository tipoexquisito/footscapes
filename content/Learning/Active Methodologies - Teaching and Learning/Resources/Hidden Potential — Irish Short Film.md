Directed by Eamonn MacMahon, 2016. Ireland. Short drama film.

---

## What It Shows

A dramatisation of a child's experience of undiagnosed dyslexia in a school environment not designed to accommodate it. The film follows the gap between the child's actual capacity and what the school is able to see — and the consequences of that gap for the child's sense of self.

---

## The Pedagogical Argument

The film's title carries the argument: the potential is present. The problem is in the system's ability to perceive and respond to it.

This connects directly to [[Universal Design for Learning]] — the principle that barriers to learning are most often environmental and structural, not inherent to the learner. When the environment changes, so does what the learner can do.

---

## In the Course

Used as a case study in the discussion of inclusive design and [[Sensory overload]] — the ways in which standard classroom environments can be systematically hostile to certain learners, not through malice but through the accumulated assumptions of a design that was never built for them.
