World Economic Forum (WEF), 2025. Annual report projecting labour market transformations over a five-year horizon.

---

## Key Findings Relevant to Education

**Top rising skills** (2025–2030): AI and big data, technological literacy, creative thinking, resilience and flexibility, systems thinking.

**Top declining roles**: data entry clerks, bank tellers, administrative assistants, postal workers — positions involving routine, rule-based cognitive tasks.

**Top growing roles**: technology specialists, sustainability officers, care economy professionals, educators and trainers.

The pattern is clear: skills that AI performs well (pattern recognition, information retrieval, rule-based processing) are declining in market value. Skills that AI performs poorly (relational intelligence, ethical judgement, contextual creativity, physical care) are rising.

---

## Manual Dexterity

The report notes that manual dexterity and precision remain high-value skills in contexts where machines cannot reliably substitute: skilled trades, care work, craft, and embodied professional practice.

Vocational education is, in this light, not a second-tier pathway. It develops skills with increasing long-term relevance.
