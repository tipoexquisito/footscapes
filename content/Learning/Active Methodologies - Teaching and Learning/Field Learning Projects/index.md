# Field Learning Projects

The field learning projects were not enrichment activities added to the programme. They were the programme.

Each project applied the [[Context4Content]] principle that learning does not need to be applied to reality — it is constructed from reality. The city, the market, the museum, the school, the painting on the wall of a bar: all of them functioned as primary learning environments. Participants were not visitors to these spaces. They were readers of them.

The projects ran at two registers. Some were designed and structured — task cards, a quiz format, a scavenger hunt, a Seesaw portfolio, a guided narrative. Others were unplanned: encounters that the city offered and the group accepted. Both registers were intentional. The coordinator confirmed it on Thursday morning: the programme had always been built this way.

---

## Structured Field Projects

**[[El Rastro]]** — Sunday morning, before the course began. La Latina, _pan con tomate_, the Sunday market. No task card. The first learning of the week arrived before learning was scheduled.

**[[Images of Madrid]]** — Monday afternoon. A walking tour structured as a quiz and scavenger hunt across eight locations: Plaza Mayor, Restaurante Botín, Plaza de la Villa, Almudena Cathedral, the Royal Palace and the 2 de Mayo plaque, Plaza de Oriente, Puerta del Sol, Km0. Participants used [[Seesaw]] for documentation and a [[Genially]] interactive map as their guide. The tour was designed to be non-linear: different routes, different experiences.

**[[Highlights of Spanish Art]]** — Wednesday afternoon at the Museo del Prado. Fourteen works across six centuries, curated into a single argument about representation, perspective and the act of looking. The meeting point was the statue of [[Goya]] at the entrance — which was itself the first work of art. The route moved through [[Fra Angelico]], [[Hieronymus Bosch]], [[Rogier van der Weyden]], [[El Greco]], [[Jacopo Tintoretto]], [[Peter Paul Rubens]], [[Diego Velázquez]] and [[Goya]], ending — after four days of accumulation — in front of [[Las Meninas]].

**[[IES Príncipe Felipe]]** — Thursday morning. A school visit as field research. Classrooms, laboratories and corridors as objects of study: UDL in the spatial layout, CLIL in the multilingual encounter with the director, cooperative learning in the student wifi programme. The frameworks made architectural.

**[[Toledo — The City of Three Cultures]]** — Friday afternoon. The city as palimpsest: Jewish, Moorish and Christian traditions in the same streets, the same buildings, the same objects. The marzipan tasted in the morning session was recognised again in the actual shop window. [[El Greco]]'s [[El entierro del conde de Orgaz]] in a darkened church. The city explained [[Context4Content]] without a framework.

## Unplanned Encounters

**[[Sala Equis]]** — Monday evening. A cinema bar in Madrid, discovered by the group. [[Metropolis]] projected on the wall above the bar, silent. The central line of the film on loop: _The mediator between the head and the hands must be the heart._ Nobody had planned it. Nobody could have.

**[[Casa de Campo]]** — Tuesday afternoon. Listed in the programme as "Autonomous Portfolio Work." What it became was a park. Space, movement, deceleration. No task, no output, no reflection prompt. The morning's frameworks continued, quietly, at a different pace.

**[[Gastronomy notes — Madrid as Memory, Ritual and Learning|Gastronomy]]** — running across the week. Bocadillos de calamares as bonus points. Cañas after the walking tour. Chocolatería San Ginés. Restaurante Botín. Los Porfiados on Thursday. Taberna Mariano in Toledo. Marzipan before Toledo, recognised again inside it. Food as [[Context4Content]], lowercase.

**[[Museo Nacional Centro de Arte Reina Sofia]]** — Saturday afternoon. Self-guided, with an audioguide as scaffold. Four to five hours across all floors: Avant-Garde Territories, [[Guernica]], the [[African Guernica]], Contemporary Art post-1975. The course had ended at midday. The learning continued until the building closed.

---

## The Logic of the Programme

The field learning projects were sequenced, not random.

Sunday began in a market before it moved into a classroom. Monday took the classroom frameworks into the streets. Wednesday took them into a museum — and had placed a painting on screen that morning as a preview of what was waiting at the entrance. Thursday took them into a school. Friday took them into a city that had been holding the week's argument in its stones for nine centuries.

Saturday took no one anywhere. The course ended and one person stayed in the Reina Sofía for four hours alone.
