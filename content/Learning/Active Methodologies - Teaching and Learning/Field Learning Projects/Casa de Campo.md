The afternoon listed portfolio work. What it became was a walk through Casa de Campo, the largest public park in Madrid. A former royal hunting estate, now a vast green corridor at the city's edge.

No task. No reflection prompt. No output. Just the city at a different pace.

After two days of dense social and cognitive intensity, the walk offered something the programme had not explicitly scheduled: space for consolidation. The conversations were quieter. The observation more lateral. The thinking less directed. The session continued, at a different pace, in a different register.
