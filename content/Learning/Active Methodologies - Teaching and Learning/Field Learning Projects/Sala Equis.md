_Monday, 4 May 2026. Calle del Duque de Alba, Madrid._

Sala Equis is not easy to categorise. It was a cinema before it was a bar, and it is still both, and neither exclusively. The building dates from the early twentieth century and spent decades as one of Madrid's adult cinemas before being converted into what it is now: a cultural space with mismatched furniture, exposed brick, films projected on the walls, and the kind of atmosphere that only exists in cities that have learned to repurpose their own history rather than erase it.

![[030-20260504-filipe-sala-equis-1.png]]
*Cool ambient at Sala Equis | Day 2 © Filipe Gama*

We arrived after the walking tour, after the cañas, after the city had already done a full day's work on us. Julia, a German colleague and Erasmus+ participant, had mentioned the place. We went.

What followed was several hours of conversation that had nothing to do with the programme and everything to do with why any of us were there. Art, culture, war, music, past experiences, the things that shape a person before they become a teacher. At some point we discovered we were both fans of dEUS, the Belgian band, and the conversation shifted into that particular register that only shared musical taste opens. I used a line I had been carrying for a while: _They told us we were the dreamers but we're the ones that don't sleep._ Proudly woke.

On the wall in front of us us, Metropolis was playing. Fritz Lang's 1927 film, silent, projected without sound into a room full of people talking. The image of a deeply technological and profoundly unequal city, its workers invisible below ground, its elites untouched above. The central argument of the film is carried in a single line: _The mediator between the head and the hands must be the heart._ It played on the wall while we talked about education.

![[032-20260504-filipe-julia-ordering-canas-sala-equis-metropolia-on-top.png]]
*Julia (DE) ordering cañas, while "Metropolis" was being projected above the bar, Sala Equis | Day 2 © Filipe Gama*

That was not planned. But it was [[Context4Content]] in its most honest form: the city offering content without being asked, the context doing the teaching while the learners are busy living.

Sala Equis is the kind of place that [[Place-based Learning]] theory describes but rarely gets to visit. A space where informal learning, social encounter, cultural circulation and shared memory happen simultaneously, without anyone designing the experience. The film was a layer. The conversation was the curriculum. The place made both possible.

![[031-20260504-filipe-sala-equis-2.png]]
*Warm neon lights at Sala Equis | Day 2 © Filipe Gama*

There is something in this that connects directly to what the week kept returning to: that learning does not require a room, a facilitator or a task card. It requires presence, attention and the willingness to stay in the conversation long enough for something to surface.

We stayed a long time.
