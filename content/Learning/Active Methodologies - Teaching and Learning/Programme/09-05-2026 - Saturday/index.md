# Dissemination & Reflection

## Overview

The course ended in the morning. The day continued.

Saturday's formal programme brought the week to its close with [[European Dimension and Dissemination]] — the same themes that had opened it on Sunday, now heard from the other side of seven days. The [[Portfolio Development]] followed: the eight-person collaborative presentation brainstormed on Tuesday built on Thursday afternoon, refined through Friday, finished in the early hours of Saturday morning. "I love a metaphor," Megan replied.

Evaluations, certificates, the formal dispersal of a temporary community of practice. 9+1 countries. Educators who had spent a week learning to see, together, what they had been doing separately for years.

![[093-20260509-group-team-diplomas.png]]

The afternoon was personal and unscheduled. An audioguide rented at the [[Museo Nacional Centro de Arte Reina Sofia|Reina Sofia]]. Four to five hours across all the floors: Avant-Garde Territories, [[Guernica]], the [[African Guernica]], Contemporary Art post-1975. The course ended at midday. The learning continued until the building closed.

## Notes on Dissemination & Reflection

The week began with a question about Europe and ended with the same question, heard differently.

On Sunday, European Dimension was a framework. By Saturday, it was a lived memory: shared meals, shared sessions, shared artworks, shared cities. The Madariaga quotation — "our Chartres, our Krakow" — had been the invisible logic of the whole week. The Prado's paintings became shared property by Wednesday. Toledo's architecture became shared by Friday. [[Guernica]] had been shared since the Padlet Arcade on Wednesday morning.

The Reina Sofia on Saturday afternoon was the week continued by other means. History does not repeat itself. But it rhymes. And when you have spent a week learning to listen for the rhyme, you hear it everywhere.
