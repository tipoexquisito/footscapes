1. **Critical Thinking**

At the critical thinking station, participants arranged nine quotes into a [[Priority Diamond]], negotiating hierarchy and justifying their choices. The activity asked for structured thinking, not right answers. Perspective-taking and metacognition were built into the instructions, not added as extras.

2. **Collaboration**

At the collaboration station, a school budget-cut scenario assigned incompatible roles: teacher, parent, student representative, principal, business manager. The [[Consensus Challenge]] introduced real conflict and asked for a negotiated proposal. It was applied social learning, and it was uncomfortable in the right ways.

3. **Creativity**

At the creativity station, the instruction was explicit: _no direct instruction, textbooks, or passive content delivery_. Participants were asked to redesign a lesson from scratch using the [[Design a Learning Experience]] prompt. The constraint was the point. Removing familiar tools forces the question of what learning actually requires.

4. **Communication**

At the communication station, the task was to explain a complex concept to a fifth-grader and then to a peer. That gap, between social fluency and academic depth, maps directly onto the [[BICS and CALP]] distinction the course would return to on Wednesday. Communicating without losing meaning is harder than it sounds, and harder still when the audience keeps changing.
