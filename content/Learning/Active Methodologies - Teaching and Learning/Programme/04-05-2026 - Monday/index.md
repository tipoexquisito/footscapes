## Notes on Educator Identity

Three questions stayed with me at the end of the day.

The first: if the sessions themselves model what good teaching looks like, what does that say about the gap between what teachers experience in training and what they reproduce in their own classrooms?

The second: the 4Cs (critical thinking, collaboration, creativity, communication) appear as consensus. But consensus in pedagogy deserves the same sceptical attention we ask students to apply everywhere else. Who defines these skills? For whom? Toward what ends?

The third, quieter: how much of what happened today was learning, and how much was belonging? And does the distinction matter?

Later, the city offered something unplanned: [[Sala Equis]]. On its wall, [[Metropolis]] — Fritz Lang's 1927 film, silent, projected without sound. The central line of the film played on loop while we talked about teaching: _The mediator between the head and the hands must be the heart._ Nobody had planned it. Nobody could have.
