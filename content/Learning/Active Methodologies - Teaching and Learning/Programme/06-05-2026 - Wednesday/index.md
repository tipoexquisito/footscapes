# Universal Design for Learning, Digital Competences & Museum Learning

## Overview

Wednesday was the day the course made its deepest argument.

The morning brought [[20260506 — Universal Design for Learning|Universal Design for Learning]] followed by [[Digital Competences and Artificial Intelligence]]. Two sessions that, on paper, could read as separate topics. In practice they were the same claim stated twice: that education without attention to access is not education at all, and that the tools we adopt carry ethical choices we are responsible for making consciously.

The afternoon brought the course's most deliberate act of [[Context4Content]]: a guided visit to the [[Highlights of Spanish Art|Museo del Prado]]. The meeting point was the statue of [[Goya]] at the main entrance. Inside, fourteen works — Fra Angelico, El Bosco, Van der Weyden, El Greco, Tintoretto, Rubens, Velázquez, Goya — formed a curated argument about representation, perspective, power and the act of looking itself.

By the end of the day, the logic of the whole week had become visible. The sessions, the city, the art: all of it had been pointing at the same thing. Learning as the careful act of reading the world.

## Notes on UDL, AI & Museum Learning

Wednesday was the day the course stopped describing pedagogy and became it.

The UDL session did not explain what it feels like to encounter content without scaffolding. It made us feel it. The AI session did not lecture about critical digital literacy. It asked us whether we wanted to be shaped by technology or to shape it. The Prado did not summarise Spanish art. It put us in a room with it and asked us to look carefully.

The statue of Goya at the entrance. The guide's voice in the Goya rooms. The painting that was already on a screen that morning. The painting that had appeared in a quiz on the third day of May, in the third week of this century, in the city where it was made.

There is a particular quality of experience when the week's threads converge in one afternoon. That is what the Prado offered on Wednesday. Not a visit. A confirmation.
