# School-Based Case Study: IES Príncipe Felipe

## Overview

Thursday moved the course off the page and into a building.

The morning was a guided visit to [[IES Príncipe Felipe]], a vocational and secondary school in Fuencarral, north Madrid. What greeted the group was not an illustration of the week's frameworks. It was the frameworks made architectural: UDL in the layout of the labs, CLIL in the negotiated multilingual encounter with the director, cooperative learning in the way students move through shared spaces, active methodologies in every room that was designed to make doing the dominant mode of learning.

Somewhere in the morning corridors, a conversation with the course coordinator confirmed what the week had been suggesting. The art, the Prado, the [[Guernica]] on a Padlet, the [[Goya]] puzzle before the Prado visit, [[Las Meninas]] on the last day of the quiz: none of it had been accidental. The programme was built on [[Context4Content]] at two registers — culture with a capital C and culture with a lowercase one. The galleries and the bocadillos. The paintings and the stickers. Both intentional.

![[070-20260507-group-team-paper-plane-statue-ies-principe-felipe.png]]
*After the visit, the team took a group picture in front of the paper plane statue, IES Príncipe Felipe | Day 5 © Team*

The afternoon brought the week's most collaborative act of synthesis. Two teams — two Portuguese, two German and four Italian participants,  — had spent the week alongside each other, divided by assignment but not by experience. At lunch they decided that presenting two separate portfolios made no sense. By the time they sat down to work, the question was no longer "what do we present?" but "what did we actually learn, together, and how do we show it?"

![[071-20260507-group-team-working-on-presentation-afternoon.png]]
*During the afternoon, the team gathered to work on their presentation. Julia (DE) was hungry | Day 5 © Team*

The answer was a mind map. And when the question arose of what to use as the visual connectors, the artworks offered themselves. [[Las Meninas]], [[The Third of May 1808]], [[The Three Graces]]: paintings that had been accumulating meaning since Sunday evening, now carrying the weight of a shared week.

## Notes on School-Based Case Study

Thursday was the day the course verified itself against a real school.

Every framework the week had introduced — UDL, CLIL, cooperative learning, SAMR, active methodologies — was either visible in the IES or visibly absent in ways that clarified what its presence would mean. The labs were not evidence of good practice. They were a prompt: what would it look like if your institution took seriously the idea that space teaches?

The conversation with the coordinator confirmed something the week had been building toward without stating directly. The art was not decoration. It was design. The choice to use [[Goya]], [[Hieronymus Bosch]], [[Diego Velázquez]] and [[Picasso]] as reference points across the week — in quizzes, in session activities, in the Prado visit, in the Padlet Arcade — reflected a deliberate reading of [[Context4Content]]: that culture operates at both registers, the canonical and the everyday, and that learning happens in the space between them.

The cañas at El Matador near Sol were the lowercase version of the same argument. Not an epilogue. Part of the text.
