# Cooperative & Language Integrated Learning

## Overview

Tuesday arrived with a small ceremony. Before the sessions began, the group voted on the best photographs from the walking tour of Madrid. The Parque del Retiro team won. A fitting start for a day that would, in many ways, be about how we see, frame and share what we encounter together.

The morning was the most theoretically grounded of the programme so far. [[20260505 - Cooperative Learning|Cooperative Learning]] brought together the theoretical roots of everything the group had been living since the beginning. [[Content and Language Integrated Learning|CLIL]] followed, insisting that language is not separate from content, and that every teacher is, to some degree, a language teacher.

The afternoon programme listed Autonomous Portfolio Work. What it became was [[Casa de Campo]], Madrid's vast urban forest, where the programme continued without an agenda. Space. Movement. Observation. Deceleration.

## Notes on Cooperative & Language Integrated Learning

Tuesday felt like the moment the course paused to look at itself in the mirror. The techniques we had been experiencing since Sunday finally received their names and their theoretical roots. Cooperative Learning is not group work. CLIL is not a method. Scaffolding is not simplification. These distinctions matter. They are the difference between doing something and understanding why it works, and for whom, and under what conditions.

The question asked during the CLIL session stays with me: how much unlearning is necessary? Every new framework implies something left behind. Every tool in the plumber's belt requires letting go of an older, less adequate one. This is uncomfortable in ways the day did not fully address, and perhaps should not. Some things need time before they can be named.

Casa de Campo offered exactly that time. No task, no output, no reflection prompt. Just a park. Just walking. And the strange, productive feeling that everything from the morning was continuing, quietly, at a different pace.
