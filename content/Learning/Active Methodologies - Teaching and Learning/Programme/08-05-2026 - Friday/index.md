# Place-Based Learning & Cultural Heritage

## Overview

The morning delivered the week's final formal session before Toledo: [[Place and Sensory Learning]], built around the cognitive science of multisensory learning and the pedagogy of place. A session about the body, about sensory memory, about what learning leaves behind when it passes through multiple channels at once. Methodologically, the fullest expression of [[Context4Content]] yet — marzipan as pedagogical material, Toledo tasted before it was walked.

In the afternoon, [[Toledo — The City of Three Cultures]].

The city where Jewish, Moorish and Christian traditions shared the same streets, the same craftspeople, the same century. Where a synagogue is built in Mudéjar style and a mosque is built inside a cathedral. Where the architecture is the argument.

## Notes on Place-Based Learning & Cultural Heritage

The week began on 3 May 2026 in Madrid, with [[Goya]]'s 3 de Mayo on a quiz screen. It ended in Toledo, where the same three traditions that Goya watched destroy each other in 1808 had spent centuries building a shared city. The architecture survived the communities. The marzipan survived the expulsions. El Greco arrived from elsewhere and made the image that defines the place.

History does not repeat itself. It rhymes. The course was designed so that the rhyme would become audible by Friday afternoon.

[[Context4Content]] was the framework. Toledo was the content. The week had always been pointing here.
