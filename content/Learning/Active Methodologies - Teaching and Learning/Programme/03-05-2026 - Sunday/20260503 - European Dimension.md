# Notes on European Dimension

> Promoting Intercultural Learning and the European Dimension in Education.

Madrid is not a neutral location. The city was chosen. So was the framing. Before any methodology was named, the course established a posture: learning is European, professional identity is shared and the room belongs to everyone equally.

The European Dimension is not a policy. It is a posture.

---

[[English Matters]] describes itself as working _[[From teachers to teachers]]_. The phrase appeared on the second slide and kept returning throughout the week. It is worth pausing on what it actually means.


![[English Matters]] 

# 9+1 countries | 14 schools | 28 participants

![[From teachers to teachers]]


The course structure followed from this. Three axes ran through the whole week: the [[European Dimension in Education|European Dimension]], the #Context4Content approach to [[Learning/Active Methodologies - Teaching and Learning/Field Learning Projects/index|Field Learning Projects]], and the [[Learning Portfolios]] as instrument of reflection and dissemination. These were not modules. They were postures, present in every session whether named or not.

The European Dimension itself was framed not as policy or bureaucracy but as a set of values made visible through practice: openness, connectedness, sharing, networking, [[Intercultural Education]]. The room in Madrid had nine participating countries plus Spain. That fact alone was part of the curriculum.

What the session established, before any methodology was introduced, was a commitment: the Erasmus+ Spirit is not incidental to the learning. It is the condition for it.
