## Day note synthesis

[[Tools for Reflection and Professional Development]] grounded the day in a different register. Not history, but identity. Not Spain, but the educator in the room.

The questions were never "what do you know?" They were "who are you as an educator?" and "what do you want to transform?" [[Learning/Active Methodologies - Teaching and Learning/Frameworks/Reflective Practice]] was introduced here as a professional competence, not an afterthought. The [[Learning Portfolios]] appeared for the first time, not as assessment, but as a living document: part reflection, part evidence, part dissemination tool. It would travel home and keep growing.

The question it kept asking was simple: what are you doing with what you are learning?

---

> "We do not learn from experience. We learn from reflecting on experience."
> John Dewey

The quote was on the first slide. It functioned less as citation and more as manifesto.

The session introduced the [[Learning Portfolios]] not as an assessment instrument but as a living document. Part reflective journal, part evidence of learning, part dissemination tool. It would travel home and keep growing after the course ended.

The evaluation framework made the course's priorities explicit. Instruments included the portfolio itself, a final survey, a participant report and a training organisation report. Criteria covered participation in group activities and debates, general understanding of content, use of field projects and cultural experiences for dissemination, and the Erasmus+ Spirit. That last criterion is worth holding: the spirit of the experience was formally assessed. Belonging, openness and engagement were not incidental. They were part of what the course was measuring.

The questions underpinning the portfolio were equally revealing. Not "what did you learn technically?" but rather: what cultural differences did you notice? What pedagogical challenges do you face? How are you using a second language? What connections will you maintain? What will you apply? What did you learn outside the classroom?

The focus was on transformation, not content acquisition. The portfolio was asking who you are becoming as an educator, not what you now know.

This is [[Learning/Active Methodologies - Teaching and Learning/Frameworks/Reflective Practice]] understood as professional competence. Metacognition not as an add-on but as a core outcome. And the dissemination expectation embedded from day one: learning that does not travel back to your school, your students, your colleagues, remains incomplete.
