# European Dimension & Foundations

## Overview

Before the course began, there was a market.

On the morning before the official start, a small Portuguese group made their way to La Latina for _pan con tomate_ and then down into El Rastro, Madrid's Sunday street market. No agenda, no task card, no Seesaw. Just a city doing what cities do on Sunday mornings, and a group of educators paying attention. That walk became the first learning of the week, before learning had been officially scheduled.

[[El Rastro]] is the first of the [[Learning/Active Methodologies - Teaching and Learning/Field Learning Projects/index|Field Learning Projects]].

The official day opened with [[20260503 - European Dimension|European Dimension]], moved through [[Images of Spain]] and [[Tools for Reflection and Professional Development]], and closed with [[Building a European Dimension]]. The sequence was not accidental. First, build belonging. Then, locate that belonging in a place. Then, ask what you will do with it when you go home.

## Notes on European Dimension & Foundations

Before anything else, there was a European Song Contest. Each country offered something. The Portuguese group opened with Barco Negro, a Fado by Amália. It didn't need explaining.

The Erasmus+ Spirit is real. It is also constructed, deliberately, session by session, meal by meal, conversation by conversation. By the end of Sunday, twelve groups of strangers had a shared reference point. That is not accidental. It is design.

And underneath everything, quietly, the commitment that would only be named on the last day: _Fiat Europa_. Make Europe. Not as bureaucracy. As daily practice, in every classroom, in every opportunity, in every lesson that opens a window instead of closing one.

The week had begun.
