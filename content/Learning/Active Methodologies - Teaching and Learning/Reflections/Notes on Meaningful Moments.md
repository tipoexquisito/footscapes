Not everything that matters can be planned. Some of the most significant learning moments of the week were incidental.

The encounter at the statue of [[Goya]] outside the [[Highlights of Spanish Art|Prado]] — before entering, before context was given — established a different relationship with the visit. Meeting the artist as a figure in the city, not just as a name on a wall.

The [[Marzipan Time — A Mindfulness Exercise]]. Attention to texture, sweetness, temperature. A lesson in what it means to actually experience something rather than process it.

The frozen [[Tableau]] technique produced a specific kind of collective embarrassment and then surprise: the body knows things the mind has not yet articulated.

The [[Audio Guide as Scaffolding]] exercise at the Reina Sofía made interpretation active. Choosing what to say about a work, and how, forces a kind of accountability to the work itself.

Walking alone in Madrid on a free afternoon — unscheduled, without group obligation — was its own pedagogy. Solitary movement through a city is a different kind of attention.

The connection during the film [[Metropolis]], the late evening that emerged unexpectedly around shared listening, belongs to the part of professional development that no report ever captures.
