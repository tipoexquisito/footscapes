*Completed as part of the Erasmus+ KA1 mobility "Innovative and Active Methodologies for Teaching and Learning", Madrid and Toledo, 3–9 May 2026.*

---

## Sunday, 3 May 2026

Before the course began, there was a market. El Rastro on a Sunday morning - no agenda, no task card. Just a city doing what cities do, and a group of educators paying attention. The official day opened with [[20260503 - European Dimension|European Dimension]], moved through [[Images of Spain]] and [[Tools for Reflection and Professional Development]], and closed with [[Building a European Dimension]]. The Erasmus+ Spirit is real. It is also constructed, deliberately - session by session, meal by meal, conversation by conversation. By the end of Sunday, a group of strangers had a shared reference point.

---

## Monday, 4 May 2026

What does it mean to teach in 2026? [[Being an Educator in 2026 and beyond]] and [[Learning/Active Methodologies - Teaching and Learning/Frameworks/21st Century Skills]] named the challenge. In the afternoon, [[Context4Content]] walked us into the streets looking for [[Images of Madrid]]. That evening, [[Sala Equis]]. On its wall, [[Metropolis]] — Fritz Lang's 1927 film, projected without sound. The central line on loop: *The mediator between the head and the hands must be the heart.* Nobody had planned it. Nobody could have.

---

## Tuesday, 5 May 2026

[[20260505 - Cooperative Learning|Cooperative Learning]] finally gave names to the practices the group had been living since Sunday. [[Content and Language Integrated Learning]] insisted that every teacher is, to some degree, a language teacher. The most productive question of the day: how much unlearning is necessary? Every new framework implies something left behind. The afternoon was [[Casa de Campo]] - no task, no output, no reflection prompt. Just a park. And the quiet sense that the morning's frameworks were continuing, at a different pace.

---

## Wednesday, 6 May 2026

Wednesday was the day the course made its deepest argument. [[20260506 — Universal Design for Learning|Universal Design for Learning]] made me feel what it is to encounter content without scaffolding. The [[Digital Competences and Artificial Intelligence]] session asked whether we wanted to be shaped by technology or to shape it. And the [[Highlights of Spanish Art|Prado]] confirmed everything. The statue of [[Goya]] at the entrance - before context was given - established a different relationship with the visit. The week's threads converged in one afternoon. Not a visit. A confirmation.

---

## Thursday, 7 May 2026

Thursday moved the course off the page and into a building. [[IES Príncipe Felipe]] was the frameworks made architectural: UDL in the layout of the labs, CLIL in the multilingual encounter with the director, cooperative learning in how students move through shared spaces. In the afternoon, eight participants from three countries decided that two separate portfolios made no sense. A mind map, built on artworks that had been accumulating meaning since Sunday.

---

## Friday, 8 May 2026

[[Place and Sensory Learning]] brought the cognitive science of the body into the room — marzipan as pedagogical material, Toledo tasted before it was walked. Then Toledo itself: the city where three cultures built the same streets, where a synagogue is built in Mudéjar style and a mosque is built inside a cathedral. The architecture is the argument. The week began in Madrid with [[Goya]]'s *2 de Mayo* on a quiz screen. It ended where the three traditions he watched destroy each other in 1808 had spent centuries building together.

---

## Saturday, 9 May 2026

The course ended in the morning. The day continued. Presentations, certificates, the formal dispersal of a temporary community of practice. Nine plus one countries. Educators who had spent a week learning to see, together, what they had been doing separately for years. The afternoon: the [[Museo Nacional Centro de Arte Reina Sofia|Reina Sofía]] alone, with an audioguide. Avant-Garde Territories, [[Guernica]], the [[African Guernica]], Contemporary Art post-1975. Four to five hours across all the floors. The course ended at midday. The learning continued.

## Professional Development

My journey started with a genuine tension: 20+ years of professional development, but I had never watched my own practice from the outside. The greatest challenge as an educator is the gap between what we know about good pedagogy and what we actually do under classroom pressure. This course offered a sustained experience of what it looks like when design and practice align: every session modelled the methodology it described. I will incorporate this through more deliberate attention to the emotional temperature of my sessions and a more conscious instinct for how I open and close them. Innovation is necessary not because novelty is a value in itself, but because the world our students are navigating has changed and our classrooms have not kept pace.

See [[Notes on Professional Development]] and [[Notes on Unlearning]].

## Cultural Understanding

Madrid offered cultural learning in layers — not as content, but as context. The architecture of daily life, the pace of the streets, the rhythm of meals, the weight of history in museums. The Spanish language was an ambient presence, not a subject to study. Cultural differences between Spain and Portugal are smaller than expected and larger than they appear. Proximity does not guarantee familiarity. The differences that matter most surface in daily behaviour: how people occupy a terrace, when they eat, what silence means in conversation.

See [[Cultural Understanding]].

## Language Development

Spending a week as a learner in a foreign-language environment produced a shift I did not anticipate. When you cannot fully express yourself in the language of the room, you listen more attentively, read body language more carefully, ask for help more honestly. These are exactly the skills we ask our students to develop, and we rarely acknowledge what it costs them to use them. The [[Stephen Krashen — The Affective Filter Hypothesis|Affective Filter]] became concrete: anxiety genuinely interferes with acquisition. A low-stakes, supportive environment is not a courtesy. It is a pedagogical requirement.

See [[Notes on Language Development]].

## Dissemination

I will share what I learned through [[index|Footscapes]] (https://tipoexquisito.github.io/footscapes/), a digital garden that documents the full week as a public educational resource. Beyond that: lessons in my own classes embedding the methodologies experienced here, conversations with colleagues using the "Do you have 5 minutes?" approach, a presentation at a school staff meeting, and a contribution to the institutional Erasmus+ report. The experience is not mine to keep. *Disseminare* — to scatter seeds. The week ended. The scattering began.

See [[Dissemination Strategy]], [[Disseminare]] and [[Micro-dissemination]].

## Meaningful Moments

On the first day, it was said that full availability for activities was expected. We remained available throughout the week. That disposition made everything else possible.

Standing in front of a bidimensional plane in Prado and being asked to look - not to identify, not to classify, but to observe the layers of meaning, the multiple perspectives compressed into a single surface. The [[Tableau]] exercise that made the body articulate what the mind had not yet formed into language. The [[Marzipan Time — A Mindfulness Exercise|Marzipan Time]] exercise: texture, temperature, sweetness — what it means to actually experience something rather than process it. [[Metropolis]] at [[Sala Equis]], projected without sound, and the late conversation that grew from it. [[Casa de Campo]] with no task, no output, no prompt - just walking in an urban forest with nowhere to be. And the [[Museo Nacional Centro de Arte Reina Sofia|Reina Sofía]] on Saturday afternoon: alone, four hours, the full collection.

But the most persistent meaningful moments were not individual. They were the group: the dynamics inside the sessions, the breaks between them, the meals that extended into the evening, the cañas and tapas cross Madrid, the conversations that did not end when the day did. A temporary community of practice that continued to practise - outside the classroom, without a schedule, without realising it was still learning. These are the moments that stayed.

See [[Notes on Meaningful Moments]] and [[Sensory overload]].

## A Final Overview

The week was designed as a complete argument. First, build belonging. Then, locate that belonging in a place. Then, ask what you will do with it when you go home. Every session modelled the methodology it described. You experienced [[20260505 - Cooperative Learning|Cooperative Learning]] while learning about Cooperative Learning. You practised [[Scaffolding]] while discussing its theory. The course was its own evidence.

What I take home is not a collection of techniques. It is a more conscious instinct for design - the difference between doing something pedagogically and understanding why it works, for whom, and under what conditions. The commitment to inclusion is not a strategy. It is a posture. [[20260506 — Universal Design for Learning|UDL]], [[Content and Language Integrated Learning|CLIL]], the [[Stephen Krashen — The Affective Filter Hypothesis|Affective Filter]], [[Place and Sensory Learning]]: all of them point at the same thing. Learning as the careful act of reading the world.

The Latin word stays with me: *disseminare* — to scatter seeds. This week did not end when the certificates were handed over. It scattered. And now it grows in classrooms, in reports, in a digital garden, and in conversations with colleagues who will ask: "What was Madrid like?" The answer is long. The answer is this journal.

See [[Fiat Humanitas]] (https://readymag.website/u1118328419/fiat-humanitas/) and [[Perspective & Reflection — Innovative and Active Methodologies for Teaching and Learning|Perspective & Reflection]].

## I like, I wish, I wonder

**I like** — I am happy that now I know that the gap between knowing about a methodology and experiencing it from the learner's side is not a detail. It is the thing. That [[Context4Content]] is not a technique but a design philosophy - that content without context is information without meaning. That the commitment named on the last day, *Fiat Europa*, is not a slogan. It is a daily practice, in every classroom, in every lesson that opens a window instead of closing one.

**I wish** — I have been inspired and plan to redesign at least one learning unit using [[20260506 — Universal Design for Learning|UDL]] principles and place-based triggers. To bring [[Context4Content]] into my own teaching context in a systematic way. To sustain [[index|Footscapes]] (https://tipoexquisito.github.io/footscapes/) as a living dissemination platform. To present what I learned to colleagues at school before the end of the academic year.

**I wonder** — I would like to know more about what happens to the relationships built this week - whether the temporary community of practice continues, and in what form. And about the question that the course raised but did not fully answer: what does it take for a school, as an institution, to make the shift that individual teachers make in a week?
