*Fiat-se Europa. Faça-se Europa.*

Let Europe be made. Not declared — made. Not as a bureaucratic structure, but as a daily practice of shared values, civic commitment, and deliberate encounter.

---

## The Proposition

The training's deepest objective was not methodological. It was ethical and civic.

The 9+1 countries in the room — Bulgaria, Croatia, Portugal, France, Czech Republic, Germany, Italy, Slovakia, Greece, Spain — were not simply a list of origins. They were Europe as a relational experience: people from different educational cultures, different languages, different histories, choosing to learn together.

That choice — repeated daily, in shared tasks, meals, museum visits, and late conversations — is what Europe is made of. Not treaties. Not institutions. This.

---

## The Connection to Fiat Humanitas

*Fiat Europa* was the course's implicit manifesto. [[Fiat Humanitas]] extends the argument: the commitment does not stop at the European border. It extends to the grammar of violence across all geographies, to the voices excluded from any canon, to the responsibility of teachers everywhere to make the world more navigable for those who arrive with less.
