[[Professional Development]] that works is not about receiving information. It is about being placed in conditions where your existing assumptions are gently but persistently tested.

This course did that. Not through confrontation, but through design: every session modelled the methodology it described. You experienced [[Cooperative Learning|Cooperative Learning]] while learning about Cooperative Learning. You practised [[Scaffolding]] while discussing its theory. The course was its own argument.

The most lasting professional shifts from this week are likely to be the ones that are hardest to name: a slightly different instinct for how to open a session, a more conscious attention to the emotional temperature of a room, a new suspicion toward transmissive teaching.
