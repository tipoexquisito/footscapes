Cultural understanding is not a destination. It is a practice of sustained attention.

Madrid offered this in layers: the architecture of daily life, the pace of the streets, the rhythm of meals, the weight of history in museums. Spain is not simply a country with a different language. It is a country with a different relationship to time, to public space, to the past.

The Spanish language itself was part of the learning environment — not as a subject, but as an ambient presence. Navigating even partially in another language produces a specific kind of humility. You stop performing fluency. You start listening more carefully.

Cultural differences between Spain and Portugal are smaller than expected and larger than they appear. Proximity does not guarantee familiarity. The differences that matter most are the ones that surface in daily behaviour: how people occupy a terrace, when they eat, what silence means in conversation.
