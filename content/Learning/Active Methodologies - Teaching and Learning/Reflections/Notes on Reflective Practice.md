The deliberate habit of using experience as a source of professional learning — not simply accumulating experience, but interrogating it.

---

## The Distinction

A teacher with twenty years of experience has twenty years of accumulated practice. That is not the same as twenty years of professional learning. Experience without reflection tends to confirm existing habits rather than develop them.

Reflective practice is the professional discipline of asking: what happened, why did it happen, what does it mean, and what would I do differently? Honestly. Repeatedly. Over time.

---

## The Frameworks

Donald Schön's *The Reflective Practitioner* (1983) distinguished between **reflection-in-action** (adjusting in real time, mid-lesson) and **reflection-on-action** (deliberate review after the fact). Both are necessary; they develop different things. See [[Learning/Active Methodologies - Teaching and Learning/Frameworks/Reflective Practice|Reflective Practice]] for the framework in full.

John Dewey's earlier argument is foundational: experience alone does not produce learning. Experience plus reflection does.

## Reflective Writing

The [[Learning Portfolios]] used during the course was a structured instrument for reflective practice: not a record of what happened, but an attempt to understand what it meant.

Writing is not the transcription of reflection. It is one of the mechanisms through which reflection happens. The act of putting experience into language forces a level of clarity that thinking alone does not require.

> "La pluma es la lengua del alma." — [[Cervantes]]

---

## The Professional Stakes

In 2026, reflective practice is not optional. The rate of change in pedagogy, technology, and the social contexts of education means that practitioners who do not reflect systematically fall behind — not in information, but in professional judgement.

Reflective practice is also a form of dissemination: what you understand about your own practice, documented and shared, contributes to professional culture. See [[Micro-dissemination]].
