> "Universal Design is the design and composition of an environment so that it can be accessed, understood and used to the greatest extent possible by all people regardless of their age, size, ability or disability."
> Centre for Excellence in Universal Design, Ireland

UDL is a set of principles for curriculum development that gives all individuals equal opportunities to learn. It minimises barriers and maximises outcomes by starting from one foundational premise: **every learner is highly variable**.

No learner is just one thing. We all have strengths and weaknesses. UDL is not a special needs programme — it is a mindset that makes education work better for everyone.

---

## Equality vs Equity

Equality assumes everyone benefits from the same support. Equity gives different support to reach the same access. UDL goes further: it removes the structural barriers so that no accommodation is needed in the first place.

> [!concept] Equality · Equity · UDL
> **Equality** gives everyone the same step. **Equity** gives taller steps to shorter people. **UDL** removes the need for steps altogether — by designing an environment that works for everyone from the start.

## The Three Principles

**[[Multiple Means of Engagement]]** — the *why* of learning. Stimulate motivation and sustained effort through flexible ways of engaging with material. Recognise that interest, persistence, and self-regulation are not fixed traits — they are conditions to be designed.

**[[Multiple Means of Representation]]** — the *what* of learning. Present information in multiple formats: text, image, audio, movement. Learners perceive and process differently; no single format reaches all.

**[[Multiple Means of Action or Expression]]** — the *how* of learning. Offer learners different ways to demonstrate what they know. Choice of assessment type is not a lowering of standards — it is recognition that performance is variable across modes.

---

## Three Levels of Implementation

UDL operates across the system, not just the classroom.

At the **macro level**: legislation, national standards, policy adoption.
At the **meso level**: school governance, institutional procedures, community links.
At the **micro level**: classroom design, teaching practices, assistive technologies, co-design with learners.

Real UDL requires alignment across all three.

## Application: Learner Variability in Practice

One session example: working with immigrant students.

Avoid segregation — place students in mainstream classes and provide support within them. Build inclusive classrooms physically (visual aids, language labels) and emotionally (valuing home cultures and languages). Use peer mentoring, breaking-the-ice activities, and resources designed to work for everyone from the start.

UDL in action is not about adding accommodations. It is about designing so that fewer accommodations are ever needed.
