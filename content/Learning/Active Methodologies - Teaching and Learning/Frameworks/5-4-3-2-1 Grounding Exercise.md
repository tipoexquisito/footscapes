A sensory grounding technique used to anchor attention in the present moment by systematically engaging each of the five senses. Useful in moments of anxiety, overload, or difficulty concentrating.

---

## The Sequence

**5** things you can see — look around and name them, internally or aloud.

**4** things you can physically feel — the chair beneath you, your feet on the floor, the texture of your clothes, the temperature of the air.

**3** things you can hear — identify sounds near and far, foreground and background.

**2** things you can smell — if nothing is immediately present, recall a smell you find calming.

**1** thing you can taste — the current taste in your mouth, or one you choose to bring to mind.

---

## Why It Works

The technique works by directing cognitive attention toward concrete, verifiable sensory experience. This interrupts ruminative or anxious thought patterns by filling working memory with present-tense, non-threatening data.

The countdown structure provides a simple cognitive scaffold for the exercise — something to follow when the capacity to follow complex instruction is itself impaired.

---

## Use in Learning Environments

The 5-4-3-2-1 exercise is well-suited to the start of a session, after a transition, or at moments of visible group disengagement. It takes two to three minutes and requires no equipment.

It connects naturally to the week's use of sensory experience as a learning anchor — the marzipan tasting, the museum visit as embodied presence, the city as sensory curriculum.
