A [[Cooperative Learning]] strategy in which each member of a home group specialises in one aspect of a learning unit, develops expertise in a temporary expert group, then returns to teach the home group.

---

## How It Works

**Home Groups** — students are divided into small, diverse groups. Each person is assigned a unique aspect of the overall topic.

**Expert Groups** — students with the same assigned aspect leave their home groups and meet with peers from other teams. Together they research, discuss, and master their piece of the material.

**Teaching and Synthesis** — students return to their home groups and take turns teaching their newly mastered content. The full picture only emerges when all contributions are in place.

---

## Why the Name

The technique is named after the jigsaw puzzle: no single piece contains the whole image, and the image only becomes visible when all pieces are assembled. Every participant is indispensable — [[Individual and Group Accountability]] is structural, not imposed.

---

## The Pedagogical Logic

Jigsaw produces high individual accountability, genuine interdependence, and peer-teaching — which sits at the top of [[The Learning Pyramid]] for retention. It also distributes authority: in the expert group phase, every participant is the most knowledgeable person in the room about their particular piece.
