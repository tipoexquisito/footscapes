# Community of Practice

A concept developed by Etienne Wenger and Jean Lave. A community of practice is a group of people who share a concern, a set of problems or a passion for a topic and deepen their knowledge and expertise by interacting on an ongoing basis.

Three defining characteristics: a shared domain of interest, a community that builds relationships through shared activities, and a shared practice — accumulated experience, tools, stories and ways of addressing recurring problems.

The phrase _from teachers to teachers_ — which opened the course on Sunday and returned throughout the week — is a community of practice claim. No hierarchy. No stage. Educators as peers, building shared knowledge across borders and bringing something back.

The Erasmus+ programme is, among other things, a deliberate infrastructure for communities of practice at European scale.
