# Educational Frameworks

> [!quote] John Dewey
> "If we teach today as we taught yesterday, we rob our children of tomorrow."

The frameworks in this garden are not a curriculum. They are lenses. Each one was introduced during the course and then tested against real contexts: a classroom in Madrid, a museum in the Prado, the streets of Toledo.

Some of these frameworks you already use without naming them. That's the point. Naming them gives you a handle. A handle gives you a way to share them with others.

> [!note] Active Methodologies
>  **Learning as something you do, not something that happens to you.**
>  
>  [[Active Methodologies|Active Methodologies]] is the umbrella. The course didn't introduce these as theory. It enacted them, session after session, so you experienced them as a learner before reflecting on them as an educator.
>  
>  Structures for cooperative work included [[Think-Pair-Share]], [[Jigsaw|Jigsaw]], [[Let's Have a Date]], [[Pass the Page]], [[Shared Reading]], [[Team Interview]] and [[Numbered Heads Together]]. [[Forming Cooperative Groups]] was its own conversation: random, teacher-selected or self-selected, each with implications for equity and dynamics. Some appear multiple times, each instance as a methodology in action rather than a description of one.
>  
>  Also worth noting: Freewrite, [[Priority Diamond]] and [[Quiz-Quiz-Trade]] as lightweight structures for reflection, critical thinking and review.

> [!note]- Cooperative Learning
>
> [[Cooperative Learning|Cooperative Learning]] rests on five pillars: positive interdependence, individual and group accountability, promotive interaction, social skills and group processing. 
>
> The theoretical anchors include Vygotsky's [[Vygotsky — Zone of Proximal Development]], Ausubel's [[Meaningful Learning]], Rogers's [[Experiential Learning]] and Krashen's [[Stephen Krashen — The Affective Filter Hypothesis]]. Gardner's [[Howard Gardner — Multiple Intelligences]] also appeared here, as a reminder that learners are not one thing.

> [!note]- Inclusive and Differentiated Learning
>
> *Designing for everyone, not adjusting for some.*
>
> [[Universal Design for Learning|Universal Design for Learning]] starts from one assumption: every learner is highly variable. The three principles of UDL, multiple means of engagement, representation and expression, were explored through a film, group discussion and a direct comparison between traditional and UDL classroom activities.
>
>**Related frameworks:** [[CLIL|CLIL]], [[Scaffolding|Scaffolding]] and [[Multiple Means of Representation|Multiple Means of Representation]]. On the CLIL side, [[BICS and CALP]] surfaced as one of the more practical distinctions of the week, alongside [[Expressing Opinions using PREP]] as a scaffolding structure for expressing and organising opinions.

> [!note]- Technology and Digital Competences
>
> *Which door are you trying to open? Which key do you need?*
>
> The [[The SAMR Model]] (Substitution, Augmentation, Modification, Redefinition) framed how technology integration is a spectrum, not a checklist. [[DigCompEdu]], the European Digital Competence Framework for Educators, and the [[SELFIE Tool for Teachers]] provided a self-assessment lens. 
>
> [[AI Literacy|AI Literacy]] was positioned as distinct from digital literacy, drawing on UNESCO, OECD and the EU AI Act frameworks, with a horizon marker: PISA 2029 will assess media and AI literacy.
>
> Tools explored in practice: Mentimeter, NotebookLM, Curipod, Padlet Arcade, Genially, Webwise. Not as mandatory tools to adopt, but as examples of [[The SAMR Model|SAMR]] levels in action.

> [!note]- Literacies
>
> *Reading the world, not just the word.*
>
> [[Visual Learning|Visual Literacy]], [[AI Literacy|AI Literacy]] and [[Media Literacy|Media Literacy]] belong together. The Prado made visual literacy concrete in ways no slide could. A Goya puzzle, a fragmented narrative, a painting reassembled before visiting the original. The question "what can you see, what do you think is happening?" is both a CLIL strategy and a visual literacy practice.
 
 > [!note]- Experiential Learning
>
> *The body learns too.*
>
> [[Place-based Learning|Place-based Learning]], [[Multisensory Learning|Sensory Learning]], [[Embodied Learning|Embodied Learning]] and [[Mindfulness|Mindfulness]] share a thread: context is not a backdrop. It is part of the learning itself.
>
> [[Tableau]] appeared as a theatre technique for embodied storytelling. The *marzapan* tasting was a mindfulness exercise and a history lesson at the same time. The 5-4-3-2-1 grounding technique and beach ball breathing were introduced as practical tools for sensory regulation, with a direct note on [[Sensory overload]] and the learners for whom sensory richness is not an asset.
>
> Toledo was not a field trip. It was a methodology.

> [!note]- Still finding their place
>
> [[Learning/Active Methodologies - Teaching and Learning/Dissemination/Dissemination|Dissemination]] and [[European Dimension in Education|Notes on European Dimension]] resist easy categorisation. They are frameworks for what happens after learning and for the larger context that makes this kind of learning possible. They will find their place.
