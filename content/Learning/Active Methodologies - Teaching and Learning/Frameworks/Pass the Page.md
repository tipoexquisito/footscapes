- Each students completes one part and passes the page to the next student.
- Then the student must check, correct and answer the next part.

---

This engaging cooperative learning strategy is widely known as **"Pass the Paper"** or **"Round Table"**. It requires students to build on each other’s ideas, verify previous work, and actively collaborate to complete an assignment together.

## How to Implement the Strategy

1. **Form Groups:** Divide students into small groups of 3 to 4 and give each group a single worksheet, graphic organizer, or diagram.
2. **First Contribution:** Student 1 completes the first section, problem, or step of the activity.
3. **Pass and Check:** Student 1 passes the page to Student 2. Student 2 must first **read, check, and correct** Student 1's work before moving to the next part.
4. **Continue the Cycle:** Student 2 completes the next part, then passes the paper to Student 3, who checks the previous answers and answers the next part. This repeats until the entire page is complete.
5. **Final Review:** Once the document makes a full circle, the entire group reviews the completed page together to reach a final consensus.

---

Why Teachers Use It

- **Accountability:** Students are less likely to disengage because their peers are directly waiting on their contribution.
- **Immediate Feedback:** Requiring students to check prior work catches mistakes early in the learning process.
- **Peer Teaching:** Students naturally explain concepts to one another as they correct and justify answers before passing the page.
