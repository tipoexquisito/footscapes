A structured sensory experience using marzipan as the object of focused, slow attention. Practised in Toledo during the course visit, at the end of a day of field learning.

---

## The Eight Steps

**1. Hold** — receive the marzipan in your palm. Do not eat it yet. Simply hold it.

**2. Observe** — look at it. Notice its colour, its shape, the surface texture, the marks left by the mould.

**3. Touch** — press it gently between your fingers. Notice the resistance, the density, the slight give.

**4. Smell** — bring it close. Breathe in. The almond, the sugar, something else underneath.

**5. Taste the surface** — touch it to your lips, then to the tip of your tongue. Before biting.

**6. Bite** — slowly. Notice the texture as it breaks, the first flood of sweetness.

**7. Chew** — deliberately. Keep attention on what is in your mouth.

**8. Reflect** — what did you notice? What changed between step 1 and step 7? What would you have missed if you had simply eaten it?

---

## The Pedagogical Argument

Mindful eating is not a wellness exercise in disguise. It is a lesson in the difference between experiencing and consuming.

The same distinction applies to learning: we frequently move through content too quickly to actually encounter it. Slowing down — holding, observing, tasting before concluding — is a transferable practice.
