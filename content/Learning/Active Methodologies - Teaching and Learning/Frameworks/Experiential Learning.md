## The Core Argument

> "We feel, therefore we learn." — [[Carl Rogers — Experiential Learning]]

Experiential learning positions direct experience as the starting point for knowledge construction. The experience alone is not sufficient — it requires structured reflection to become learning. The cycle moves from concrete experience to reflective observation, from abstract conceptualisation to active experimentation, and back to new experience.

This is the logic formalised by David Kolb (1984) in his Experiential Learning Cycle, drawing on the earlier work of Rogers, Dewey, and Lewin.

## Connections in the Course

The entire week was structured as experiential learning: the museum visits, the city walks, the cooperative tasks, and the reflective writing were all designed as experiences before they were content delivery.
