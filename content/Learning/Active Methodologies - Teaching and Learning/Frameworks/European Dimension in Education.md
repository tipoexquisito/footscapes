A framework for understanding education as a shared European practice — not a bureaucratic category but a posture, a set of values made visible through daily professional decisions.

---

## What It Is

The European Dimension in education is not Erasmus+. It is not a funding programme or a policy framework. Those are instruments. The European Dimension is the commitment that runs through them: that teachers across different national systems share something — values, responsibilities, challenges — and that learning from each other is both possible and necessary.

> "The room in Madrid had nine participating countries plus Spain. That fact alone was part of the curriculum."

---

## Three Axes

Three dimensions of the European Dimension were present throughout the week, whether named or not:

**Intercultural learning** — genuine encounter with different pedagogical cultures, not tourism of difference. The Bulgarian teacher and the Portuguese teacher bring different inherited assumptions about what a classroom is. That gap is not a problem to manage; it is the resource.

**Professional solidarity** — the Erasmus+ spirit is the recognition that teachers are not isolated practitioners. What works in one classroom, documented and shared, can work in another. See [[Learning/Active Methodologies - Teaching and Learning/Dissemination/Dissemination|Dissemination]] and [[Disseminare]].

**Shared values** — openness, inclusion, democratic participation, critical thinking, and the commitment to learners' dignity regardless of background. These are not European inventions, but Europe chose to name them as founding commitments. [[Fiat Europa]] is the daily practice of that choice.

---

## The Civic Dimension

European education is not neutral. The European Dimension implies a position: that education should produce people capable of living alongside difference, thinking critically, and contributing to shared democratic life.

This connects to [[AI Literacy]] (navigating the information environment), [[Healthy scepticism]] (resisting manipulation), and the broader argument of [[Fiat Humanitas]] — that the commitment does not stop at the European border.
