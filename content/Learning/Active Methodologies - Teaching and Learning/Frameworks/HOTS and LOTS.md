# HOTS and LOTS

Higher-Order Thinking Skills (HOTS) and Lower-Order Thinking Skills (LOTS) derive from [[Bloom's Taxonomy]]. In [[Content and Language Integrated Learning|CLIL]] and active methodologies, task design attends explicitly to this distinction: lower-order tasks (recall, comprehension) build the foundation; higher-order tasks (analysis, evaluation, creation) are where deeper learning happens.

The point is not to avoid LOTS but to ensure HOTS are consistently present and scaffolded. A CLIL lesson that only asks students to recall vocabulary is not yet CLIL.
