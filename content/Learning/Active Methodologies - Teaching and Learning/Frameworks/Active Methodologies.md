*Methodology through experience. Pedagogy by enactment.*

> "If we teach today as we taught yesterday, we rob our children of tomorrow." (John Dewey)
> "New knowledge connects to prior experience." (David Ausubel)

Active methodologies shift the learner from recipient to agent. They are not a single framework but a family of approaches sharing a common premise: learning happens by doing, making, reflecting, and relating — not by receiving.

The course was itself a demonstration. Every session used at least one active methodology, and the city, the museums, and the streets were the curriculum.

---

## Map of Methodologies Used

**[[Cooperative Learning]]** — structured interdependence. Jigsaw, Think-Pair-Share, peer discussion, collaborative interpretation.

**[[Inquiry-Based Learning]]** — learner as investigator. Artwork interpretation, historical inference, visual reading, guided discovery.

**[[Place-based Learning]]** — learning *in* and *through* place, not *about* it. Images of Madrid walking tour, Museo del Prado, Toledo, Casa de Campo.

**Contextual Learning** — content shaped by context. See [[Context4Content]].

**[[Experiential Learning]]** — learning by doing and reflecting (Kolb). Visits, walks, observation, role-play, sensory engagement.

**Embodied Learning** — learning that passes through the body. [[Tableau]], gesture, frozen scenes, breathing, [[Mindfulness]].

**[[Multisensory Learning]]** — engaging multiple sensory channels for more durable encoding. Sound, smell, space, body, vision, movement.

**[[Visual Learning]]** — art as methodology, not content. Composition, media literacy, representation.

**Reflective Learning** — learning made conscious through portfolio, prompts, notebook, and continuous reflection.

**[[Gamification]]** — quiz, bonus challenges, group dynamics.

**Informal Learning** — even autonomous time functioned as learning. Urban drift, exploration, unstructured encounter.

---

## The Hidden Methodology

The strongest insight from the course: the deepest learning did not happen in the slides. It happened in the Prado, in Toledo, in Casa de Campo — in unexpected connections between art, space, and experience.

Perhaps the overarching methodology was curation. The teacher as curator of learning experiences — selecting, sequencing, contextualising, and leaving space for the learner's own meaning-making.
