A model of average retention rates associated with different learning methods, commonly attributed to the National Training Laboratories (NTL) Institute, USA.

---

## The Pyramid

| Method | Average Retention |
|--------|------------------|
| Lecture | 5% |
| Reading | 10% |
| Audiovisual | 20% |
| Demonstration | 30% |
| Discussion group | 50% |
| Practice by doing | 75% |
| Teaching others | 90% |

The model distinguishes **passive** methods (lecture, reading, audiovisual) from **active** methods (discussion, practice, teaching others). Retention rates increase as learners become more active participants in their own learning.

---

## A Note on the Evidence

The specific percentages circulate widely but their empirical basis is contested. No peer-reviewed study has validated these exact figures. The NTL itself has not produced the original research documentation.

The model remains pedagogically useful as a directional argument — active learning produces more durable understanding than passive reception — even if the precise numbers should not be treated as scientific fact.

This is itself a lesson in **healthy scepticism**: a model being widely cited is not the same as it being well evidenced. See [[Healthy scepticism]] and [[AI Literacy]].
