Situated Learning, developed by Jean Lave and Etienne Wenger (1991), argues that knowledge is inseparable from the context in which it is learned and used. Learning is not the transfer of abstract knowledge into a neutral mind — it is participation in communities of practice, embedded in specific social and cultural settings.

The implication for education: authentic contexts produce more durable and transferable learning than decontextualised instruction.

> "Knowledge is inseparable from activity and the context, culture, and community in which it is developed and deployed."
> — Lave & Wenger, *Situated Learning* (1991)

---

## Connection to the Course

The entire week in Madrid was an exercise in situated learning. Visiting the Prado is not the same as reading about it. Standing in Toledo is not the same as watching a documentary. The learning was situated because it happened in the place it was about.
