David Ausubel (1918–2008), American educational psychologist, argued that learning is only meaningful when new information connects to something the learner already knows.

---

## The Core Argument

> "The most important single factor influencing learning is what the learner already knows. Ascertain this and teach accordingly."

New knowledge does not arrive in an empty container. It is interpreted through existing cognitive structures. When new information connects to prior knowledge in a meaningful way, it is assimilated and retained. When it cannot connect, it is memorised without understanding — and quickly lost.

---

## The Learner at the Centre

The implication for teaching is clear: start with the learner's existing knowledge, not with the curriculum's logical structure. The question is not "what is the correct sequence of this content?" but "what does this learner already bring, and where does this connect?"

The teacher becomes a **facilitator** — someone who builds bridges between what is known and what is new.
