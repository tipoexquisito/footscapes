> "Co-operation is working together to achieve common goals."
> "Students work together to maximise their own and each other's learning."
> Johnson and Johnson; Spencer Kagan

Cooperative learning is a form of active learning in which students perform specific tasks in small groups organised by the teacher. It draws on multiple learning theories — [[Howard Gardner — Multiple Intelligences]] (Gardner), [[Meaningful Learning]] (Ausubel), [[Vygotsky — Zone of Proximal Development]] (Vygotsky), [[Experiential Learning]] (Rogers), and the [[Stephen Krashen — The Affective Filter Hypothesis]] (Krashen) — and formalises their social and collaborative dimensions.

---

> [!concept] Cooperative vs Collaborative
> **Collaborative learning** puts learners together and hopes something useful happens. **Cooperative learning** structures the interaction — roles, interdependence, accountability, reflection — so that productive learning is not left to chance.

## The Five Pillars

The [[5 Pillars of Cooperative Learning]] define what makes a group truly cooperative rather than simply working in proximity.

**[[Positive Interdependence]]** — learners succeed together or not at all. Each person's contribution matters to the group's outcome.

**[[Individual and Group Accountability]]** — each learner is responsible for their own learning and for contributing to the group's shared goal.

**[[Promotive Interaction]]** — learners actively support each other's understanding, explain, encourage, and challenge.

**[[Social Skills]]** — communication, leadership, trust, conflict resolution. These are taught explicitly, not assumed.

**[[Group Processing]]** — groups regularly reflect on how well they are working together and what could improve.

## The Learning Pyramid

Cooperative learning consistently produces higher retention rates than passive reception. The [[The Learning Pyramid]] (National Training Laboratories) situates teaching others and group discussion at the top of retention — the same modes cooperative structures are designed to activate.
