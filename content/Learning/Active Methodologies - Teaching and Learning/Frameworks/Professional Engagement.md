From the [[DigCompEdu]] framework, Area 1: Professional Engagement. Four competency areas for using digital technologies to enhance professional practice beyond the classroom.

---

## 1.1 — Organisational Communication

Using digital technologies to enhance communication with learners, families, and third parties. Contributing to collaborative organisational communication strategies.

The shift here is from broadcasting information to designing communication: choosing the right channel, register, and format for the intended audience.

---

## 1.2 — Professional Collaboration

Using digital technologies to engage in collaboration with other educators — sharing knowledge and experience, and collaboratively developing pedagogical practice.

This is the dissemination impulse expressed as a daily professional habit: what you learn from your colleagues, you share. What you develop together, you document.

See [[Learning/Active Methodologies - Teaching and Learning/Dissemination/Dissemination|Dissemination]] and [[Disseminare]].

---

## 1.3 — Reflective Practice

Individually and collectively reflecting on, critically assessing, and actively developing one's own digital pedagogical practice and that of the wider educational community.

Reflective practice is not journal-keeping for its own sake. It is the professional discipline of using experience as data — and adjusting accordingly. See [[Learning/Active Methodologies - Teaching and Learning/Frameworks/Reflective Practice]].

---

## 1.4 — Digital Continuous Professional Development

Using digital sources and resources for ongoing professional learning and development.

The professional who stops learning has already begun to fall behind. In 2026, this is not an abstract warning.
