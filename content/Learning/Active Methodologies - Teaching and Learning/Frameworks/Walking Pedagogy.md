# Walking Pedagogy

Walking pedagogy treats the walk — through a city, a landscape, a museum — as a pedagogical structure, not a transit between learning moments.

Walking changes the relationship between learner and environment. The pace slows, peripheral attention widens, unexpected encounters become possible. The learner is not reading about a place; they are moving through it.

In this course, the [[Images of Madrid]] walking tour was the most structured instance. [[El Rastro]] on Sunday morning and [[Casa de Campo]] on Tuesday afternoon were the unstructured ones. All three were pedagogically intentional.
