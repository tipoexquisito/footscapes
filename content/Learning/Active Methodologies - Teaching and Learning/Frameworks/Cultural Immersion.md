# Cultural Immersion

Cultural immersion describes deep, sustained engagement with a cultural context — its spaces, practices, objects, languages and rhythms — as a condition for learning rather than as a supplement to it.

It is not tourism. Tourism moves through a culture. Immersion enters it at sufficient depth for the familiar to become strange and the strange to become legible.

In this course, immersion operated at multiple scales: the city as classroom, the museum as text, the market as field site, the meal as cultural object. The difference between a visit to Madrid and an immersive week in Madrid is the accumulation of encounters that begin to form a pattern — the same Goya appearing in a quiz, a Prado puzzle, a Padlet, a portfolio slide deck.
