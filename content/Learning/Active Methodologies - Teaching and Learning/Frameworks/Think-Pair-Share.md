**Think-Pair-Share** is a collaborative learning strategy where participants independently reflect on a prompt (Think), discuss their ideas with a partner (Pair), and present their synthesized thoughts to the broader group (Share). It maximizes engagement and builds confidence in a safe environment.

---

### The 3-Step Process

1. **Think:** The instructor poses an open-ended question. Participants reflect and gather their thoughts independently, often jotting down notes.
2. **Pair:** Participants turn to a partner to compare notes, debate viewpoints, and synthesize their answers into a joint response.
3. **Share:** Pairs present their combined insights to the larger group, allowing for a structured and inclusive collective discussion

### Why It Works

- **Maximizes Participation:** It ensures every single person is involved rather than just the most vocal participants.
- **Builds Confidence:** Rehearsing a response with one partner reduces the anxiety of speaking directly to a large group.
- **Enhances Comprehension:** It gives time to process information critically and form deeper insights.
