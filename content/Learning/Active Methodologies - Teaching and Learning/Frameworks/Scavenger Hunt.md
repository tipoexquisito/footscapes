A cooperative learning technique in which groups search a physical or digital space to locate, identify, or collect specific items or information. Used as an activation strategy, a field learning structure, or an icebreaker.

---

## The Pedagogical Logic

A scavenger hunt replaces passive reception with active discovery. Participants must look, decide, move, and collaborate — producing the kind of engaged, embodied learning that a lecture cannot.

In a museum or city context, the hunt gives learners a purpose and a structure for observation: instead of walking past without looking, they are looking for something. The constraint of the task focuses attention.

---

## In the Course

Used during field learning visits as a structured attention exercise: participants were given specific works to find, questions to answer through careful looking, or connections between objects to identify.
