# Multimodal Literacy

Multimodal literacy extends the concept of literacy beyond the written word. It describes the capacity to read, interpret and produce meaning across multiple modes: text, image, sound, gesture, movement, spatial arrangement.

In educational contexts, multimodal literacy becomes relevant wherever learning involves more than written language — which, in practice, is almost everywhere. The city, the museum, the film, the infographic, the soundscape: all require multimodal reading.

In this course, multimodal literacy appeared most explicitly in [[Visual Learning]] and in the [[Place and Sensory Learning]] session.
