Learning that connects new information to what the learner already knows, producing durable understanding rather than temporary memorisation.

---

## The Distinction

Rote learning deposits information in memory without connecting it to existing knowledge. It is retrieved only as long as the memory trace is fresh, and it does not generalise.

Meaningful learning integrates new knowledge into existing cognitive structures. It can be retrieved in new contexts, applied to unfamiliar problems, and built upon.

> "The most important single factor influencing learning is what the learner already knows. Ascertain this and teach accordingly." — [[David Ausubel — Meaningful Learning]]

---

## The Conditions for Meaningful Learning

Three conditions are necessary. The material must be logically meaningful — it must have an inherent structure that makes connection possible. The learner must have relevant prior knowledge to connect it to. The learner must choose to engage meaningfully — not simply memorise.

All three are design responsibilities as much as learner responsibilities.
