Intercultural education goes beyond recognising cultural diversity. It asks learners to engage with difference as a productive force — to understand other cultural logics on their own terms, not only in relation to a dominant norm.

It is not tolerance (accepting difference from a position of superiority). It is encounter: meeting other perspectives with genuine curiosity and willingness to be changed by them.

---

## In the Course

The Erasmus+ context made intercultural education structural rather than optional. Participants came from different countries, working languages, institutional cultures, and pedagogical traditions. The diversity was the curriculum.

Toledo deepened this: the city itself is an argument for intercultural coexistence. Christian, Muslim, and Jewish communities built, inhabited, and transformed the same urban space across centuries. What remains is layered, contested, and irreducible to a single narrative. See [[Toledo — The City of Three Cultures]].

Fiat Humanitas engages this directly — through Goya, Picasso, Feni, and Al-Hawajri, works from different cultural positions that rhyme across time. See [[Fiat Humanitas]].
