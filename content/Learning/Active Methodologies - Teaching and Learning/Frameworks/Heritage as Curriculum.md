# Heritage as Curriculum

The principle that cultural and historical heritage — buildings, objects, places, practices — can function as primary learning content rather than as backdrop or illustration.

Heritage as Curriculum shifts the question from "how do we teach about this heritage?" to "what does this heritage teach?" The object, the city, the monument is not a resource for the curriculum. It is the curriculum.

The distinction matters pedagogically. When heritage is illustration, learning happens before the visit and the visit confirms it. When heritage is curriculum, learning happens in encounter — through observation, interpretation and reflection on what is actually present.

In this course, heritage appeared at two registers: canonical (the Prado, Toledo, Las Meninas) and everyday (bocadillos de calamares, sticker culture, the pedestrian traffic light that sounds like birds). Both were intentional. Both taught.
