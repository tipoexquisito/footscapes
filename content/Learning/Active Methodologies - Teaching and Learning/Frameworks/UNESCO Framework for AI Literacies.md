Published by UNESCO as part of its broader work on AI in education. The framework maps competencies for both students and teachers across five levels of AI literacy.

---

## For Students

The framework identifies five progressive competency levels:

**Level 1 — AI Awareness**: Understanding that AI exists, what it does, and where it appears in daily life.

**Level 2 — AI Use**: Ability to use AI tools effectively, evaluate their outputs critically, and understand their limitations.

**Level 3 — AI Understanding**: Conceptual understanding of how AI systems work — training data, algorithms, outputs, biases.

**Level 4 — AI Evaluation**: Capacity to assess AI applications ethically and critically: fairness, transparency, accountability, societal impact.

**Level 5 — AI Creation**: Ability to design, build, or meaningfully contribute to AI systems and their governance.

---

## For Teachers

The teacher framework runs in parallel, with additional emphasis on:

Integrating AI tools into pedagogical practice with critical awareness. Designing learning experiences that develop students' AI literacies. Modelling critical and ethical engagement with AI outputs. Understanding institutional and policy dimensions of AI in education.
