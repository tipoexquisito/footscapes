A framework for the competencies learners need to thrive in contemporary professional and civic life. Commonly grouped into four clusters: the 4Cs of Learning, Literacy Skills, Life Skills, and Career Skills.

---

## The 4Cs of Learning

**Critical Thinking** — working together toward a shared goal; combining individual strengths through collaborative structures. The social dimension of knowledge construction.

**Creativity** — the ability to evaluate evidence, separate fact from opinion, and resist the pull of uncritical acceptance. Asking *why*, *how do we know*, and *whose perspective is this*.

**Communication** — conveying ideas with clarity and purpose; reading the audience and adjusting register, format, and complexity accordingly.

**Collaboration** — generating original ideas, approaches, and solutions; thinking outside established conventions; taking creative risks.

## Literacy Skills

**Information Literacy** — locating, evaluating, and applying information from multiple sources, including the capacity to distinguish reliable from unreliable content. Connects to [[Healthy scepticism]] and [[AI Literacy]].

**Media Literacy** — understanding how media messages are constructed, what interests they serve, and how they shape perception. Connects to [[What is truth in 2026?]] and [[Las Meninas]].

**Technology Literacy** — digital proficiency; the capacity to use tools independently and to conduct independent research. Connects to [[Facilitating Learners' Digital Competence]] and the five DigComp areas.

---

## Life Skills

**Flexibility** — adapting to new contexts, navigating social situations with appropriate register, and maintaining productive working relationships across difference.

**Initiative** — intrinsic motivation, personal work ethic, and the capacity to act without waiting for permission or instruction.

**Productivity** — managing time and attention; completing work to standard within available constraints.

**Leadership** — setting goals for a group, guiding collaborative effort, and taking responsibility for shared outcomes.

---

## Expression

The ability to demonstrate learning and communicate understanding in multiple formats — written, verbal, visual, spatial, and performative. Connects to [[Universal Design for Learning]] (multiple means of action and expression) and [[Howard Gardner — Multiple Intelligences]].

---

## Connections in the Course

The 21st Century Skills framework ran through the entire week: cooperative tasks built collaboration and communication; museum visits required critical and creative thinking; reflective writing and multimodal outputs developed expression.
