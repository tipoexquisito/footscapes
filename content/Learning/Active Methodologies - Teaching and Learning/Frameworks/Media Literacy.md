The capacity to access, analyse, evaluate and create media in a variety of forms. Media literacy goes beyond the ability to use media tools — it includes critical understanding of how media shapes perception, constructs narrative and carries ideological weight.

Media literacy overlaps with [[AI Literacy]] and [[Visual Learning]]: all three involve the ability to read representations critically rather than passively.

Key questions in media literacy education:
- Who produced this message, and why?
- What techniques are used to attract and hold attention?
- What values, perspectives or assumptions are embedded?
- What is absent or omitted?
- How might different audiences interpret this differently?

In the context of this course, media literacy connects to the analysis of visual art as political communication — particularly in the work of Goya, Picasso and the readings of [[Guernica]] and [[African Guernica]].

PISA 2029 will assess media and AI literacy alongside traditional academic competences.
