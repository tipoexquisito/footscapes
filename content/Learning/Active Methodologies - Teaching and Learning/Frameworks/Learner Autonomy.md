# Learner Autonomy

Learner autonomy describes the capacity — and willingness — of a learner to take active responsibility for their own learning: setting goals, monitoring progress, choosing strategies, and reflecting on outcomes.

It is not independence from the teacher. It is a growing competence for self-direction within a supported structure. The teacher's role shifts accordingly: from controller of content to designer of conditions in which autonomy can develop.

In this course, autonomy appeared in several forms: the autonomous portfolio work on Tuesday afternoon, the self-guided visit to the Reina Sofía on Saturday, the IES Príncipe Felipe's _alumnos wifi_ programme. Each was a different point on the same axis — from structured support toward independent agency.

> [!connection] Relation to UDL and CLIL
> [[Universal Design for Learning]] creates the conditions for autonomy by removing barriers to access. [[Content and Language Integrated Learning|CLIL]] builds it by gradually withdrawing [[Scaffolding]] as competence grows. Autonomy is the destination both are pointing toward.
