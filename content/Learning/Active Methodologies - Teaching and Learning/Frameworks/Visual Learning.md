Visual Learning is not simply learning that uses images. It is the development of the capacity to read images — to extract meaning from composition, colour, symbol, spatial arrangement, and absence.

In the context of this course, art was used as methodology, not decoration. The Prado and the Reina Sofía were field sites, not museums to visit. The question was never "what is this?" but "what does this do, and how?"

---

## Visual Literacy

Visual literacy is the ability to interpret, analyse, and produce meaning through visual media. It includes:

- reading composition and spatial relationships
- recognising symbol, allegory, and narrative convention
- questioning representation: who is shown, and how? Who is absent?
- understanding the historical and social context of an image
- producing visual work with intention

---

## In Practice

**At the Prado**: slow looking at a single work. Sitting with a painting long enough for the familiar to become strange.

**Images of Madrid** (Day 1): photography as a tool for observation and analysis. The camera as a thinking instrument, not a recording device.

**Guernica and its dialogues**: reading Picasso's visual language; reading Dumile Feni and Al-Hawajri in response to it. Visual argument across time and geography.

---

> [!concept] Art as methodology
> The shift from "art as content" to "art as methodology" is significant. When learners are asked not to learn about art but to learn *through* art — to use it as a tool for thinking — the work changes. So does the learner.
