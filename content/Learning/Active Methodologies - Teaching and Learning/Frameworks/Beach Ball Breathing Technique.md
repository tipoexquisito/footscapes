A visual breathing regulation technique using the image of a beach ball being inflated and deflated as a guide for breath.

---

## The Technique

Imagine holding a deflated beach ball in your hands.

Breathe in slowly through your nose — picture the ball expanding in your hands as your lungs fill. Feel your chest and belly rise with it.

Hold briefly at the top.

Breathe out slowly through your mouth — the ball deflates, your hands come closer together, your body releases.

Repeat at a slow, comfortable pace for four to six cycles.

---

## Why the Visualisation Helps

The beach ball image gives the breath a concrete, trackable form. For learners who find pure breath-focus difficult — common in anxiety, ADHD, or trauma responses — the visual object provides a cognitive anchor that makes the practice more accessible.

The technique is particularly suitable with younger learners, or as an introduction to breathing regulation for adults unfamiliar with mindfulness practice.
