From the [[DigCompEdu]] framework (European Framework for the Digital Competence of Educators), Area 6: Facilitating Learners' Digital Competence.

---

## 6.1 — Information and Media Literacy

Supporting learners in accessing, finding, filtering, and critically evaluating digital information and media content.

This includes developing the capacity to distinguish reliable from unreliable sources, understand algorithmic curation, and recognise how platforms shape what information is visible.

Connects to [[AI Literacy]], [[Healthy scepticism]], and [[What is truth in 2026?]].

---

## 6.2 — Digital Communication and Collaboration

Supporting learners in using digital tools to communicate, collaborate, share, and participate in online communities responsibly and effectively.

This covers netiquette, digital presence, collaborative platforms, and the ethics of digital interaction.

---

## The Pedagogical Responsibility

DigCompEdu Area 6 rests on a specific argument: teachers are not only responsible for *using* technology in their own practice. They are responsible for developing their students' capacity to navigate a digital world independently, critically, and ethically.

The classroom is the first formal space where learners encounter these skills as an explicit subject of learning — not as background infrastructure but as a civic competence.
