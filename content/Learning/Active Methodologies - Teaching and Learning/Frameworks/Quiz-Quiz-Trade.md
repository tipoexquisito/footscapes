**Quiz-Quiz-Trade** is a highly interactive, cooperative learning routine where students use flashcards or notes to quiz each other, coach peers, and trade cards before rotating to new partners. This kinesthetic activity boosts information retention and allows students to socialize while reviewing course material.

## How to Play (The 7-Step Process)

1. **Prepare Cards:** Create a set of flashcards or notes. Put a question, vocabulary term, or prompt on one side, and the answer or definition on the back.
2. **Distribute:** Give every student exactly one card.
3. **Stand Up & Pair Up:** Students stand up, raise their hands, and find the nearest classmate who is also raising a hand.
4. **Quiz:** Partner A shows the question/term to Partner B. Partner B tries to answer. Partner A provides helpful clues if Partner B is stuck, and praises them when they get it right.
5. **Switch Roles:** The partners swap roles so that Partner B quizzes Partner A.
6. **Trade Cards:** Once both students have answered and coached each other, they trade flashcards.
7. **Repeat:** Students raise their hands to find a new partner and repeat the process.

## Best Practices & Variations

- **Low-Pressure Prompts:** Instead of vocabulary terms, try using Post-it notes with discussion prompts (e.g., "Write three ways to prioritize mental health"). Students share their answers, swap Post-its, and collect new ideas from multiple classmates.
- **Partner Formations:** To manage movement, you can have students form two face-to-face lines. After each trade, one entire line simply shifts one step to the right to pair up with a brand-new partner.
- **Timer Use:** Use a timer or play music to signal when it is time for students to find a new partner and keep the room's energy high.
