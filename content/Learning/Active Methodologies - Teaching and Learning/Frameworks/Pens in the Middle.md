A cooperative learning technique that regulates turn-taking and ensures every participant contributes. Simple to set up, powerful in its effects.

---

## How It Works

Groups of three to five participants sit around a table. Each person places their pen (or another small object) in the centre.

The rule: when you speak, you take your pen from the centre. When you have finished speaking, you return it. You cannot speak again until everyone else has placed their pen in the centre at least once — that is, until everyone has contributed.

---

## What It Does

The rule makes participation visible and equitable. It prevents dominant speakers from monopolising the floor without requiring the facilitator to intervene. It creates natural pauses that make space for quieter participants. It distributes accountability: each person is both a speaker and a keeper of the group's balance.

The physical act of picking up and returning the pen makes the social contract concrete and legible to everyone in the group.

---

## Debrief

After the task: what did you notice about the pattern of participation? Who spoke first, who waited? Did the rule change how you listened? What happened in the silences?
