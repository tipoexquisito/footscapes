An educational approach that uses the local environment — the immediate community, landscape, city, or cultural context — as the starting point and ongoing resource for learning.

---

## The Core Argument

Place-based learning positions context as curriculum. The street, the museum, the market, the neighbourhood, and the city are not settings for learning — they are the subject of learning.

This is not fieldwork as supplement. It is fieldwork as foundation.

---

## The Pedagogical Logic

When learners encounter knowledge in the place where it is meaningful — studying urban history in a city square, encountering art in a museum, tasting food that carries cultural memory — the learning is contextualised, embodied, and more likely to endure.

Place provides what the classroom, by design, cannot: irreducible specificity. The thing itself, in its actual location, carrying its actual weight.
