From the [[DigCompEdu]] framework (European Framework for the Digital Competence of Educators), Area 5.

---

## 5.1 — Accessibility and Inclusion

Using digital technologies to ensure all learners can access learning resources and activities, regardless of physical, cognitive, or linguistic barriers.

This goes beyond compliance. Accessibility is a design commitment: the learning environment is built to include, not adapted after the fact. Connects to [[Universal Design for Learning]].

---

## 5.2 — Differentiation and Personalisation

Using digital tools to adapt the pace, level, and approach of learning to individual learner needs. Technology here is a means of scaling differentiation — giving each learner a pathway that matches their current level of challenge and support.

Connects to [[The Four Zones of Teaching and Learning]] and [[Vygotsky — Zone of Proximal Development]].

---

## 5.3 — Active Engagement

Using digital technologies to foster learners' active participation in the learning process — moving from consumption to production, from passive reception to active meaning-making.

This is the SAMR Modification/Redefinition space. See [[The SAMR Model]].

---

## The Underlying Principle

Area 5 rests on the conviction that digital competence in the teacher is not sufficient. The goal is not a digitally fluent educator managing a passive class. The goal is learners who are digitally capable, actively engaged, and increasingly autonomous.
