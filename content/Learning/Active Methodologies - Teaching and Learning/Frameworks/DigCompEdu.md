The European Framework for the Digital Competence of Educators, published by the European Commission's Joint Research Centre in 2017, provides a common reference for what digital competence means for teaching professionals across Europe.

DigCompEdu describes six competence areas:

1. Professional Engagement — using digital technologies for professional collaboration, communication and development
2. Digital Resources — sourcing, creating and sharing digital content
3. Teaching and Learning — managing and orchestrating digital technologies in teaching
4. Assessment — using digital tools to enhance assessment strategies
5. Empowering Learners — using digital technologies to increase inclusion, personalisation and active engagement
6. Facilitating Learners' Digital Competence — enabling learners to develop their own digital competence

The framework is organised across six proficiency levels, from Newcomer to Pioneer. It is not a checklist but a developmental continuum.
