The OECD framework for AI literacy draws on DigComp, UNESCO's work, and Digital Promise research. It maps AI literacy across three dimensions.

---

## Three Dimensions

**Knowledge** — understanding what AI is and how it works: machine learning, training data, model outputs, probabilistic reasoning, and the difference between narrow and general AI.

**Skills** — the practical capacity to use AI tools critically and effectively: prompting, evaluating outputs, identifying bias, understanding limitations, and applying AI to real-world problems.

**Attitudes** — the values and dispositions needed to engage with AI as a citizen and professional: openness to learning, ethical awareness, critical scepticism, and a sense of personal and civic responsibility toward AI's societal effects.

---

## The Interdependence of the Three

Knowledge without skills remains theoretical. Skills without knowledge produce uncritical use. Both without appropriate attitudes risk harm at scale. The framework treats the three as inseparable.
