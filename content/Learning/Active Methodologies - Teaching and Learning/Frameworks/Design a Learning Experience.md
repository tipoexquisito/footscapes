A cooperative design challenge in which groups redesign an existing lesson using active methodologies, removing direct instruction as the primary mode.

---

## The Challenge

Groups receive the prompt: *Redesign a lesson from your own teaching practice so that learners construct understanding themselves — without the teacher explaining the content at the front.*

The constraint is intentional. The absence of direct instruction forces participants to think from the learner's perspective: what would they need to encounter, explore, discuss, or create in order to arrive at the intended understanding?

---

## The Four-Step Process

**Step 1 — Deconstruct** — identify the existing lesson: what is it trying to achieve? What do learners currently do? Where is the direct instruction concentrated?

**Step 2 — Redesign** — working as a group, replace the direct instruction with active structures: inquiry tasks, cooperative techniques, visual or embodied activities, real-world problems.

**Step 3 — Sequence** — arrange the activities into a coherent learning journey. Consider pacing, transition, variety of mode, and the emotional arc of the experience.

**Step 4 — Pitch** — each group presents their redesigned lesson in one minute. No slides required. The constraint of one minute forces clarity.

---

## The Debrief Question

What did you have to give up to make this work? And was it actually worth giving up?
