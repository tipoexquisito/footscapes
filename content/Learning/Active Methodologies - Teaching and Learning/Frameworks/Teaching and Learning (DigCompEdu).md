From the [[DigCompEdu]] framework, Area 3: Teaching and Learning. Four competency areas for integrating digital technologies into pedagogical practice.

---

## 3.1 — Teaching

Planning and implementing digital tools and resources in the teaching process to enhance effectiveness. Managing digital teaching interventions appropriately. Experimenting with and developing new formats and pedagogical methods.

The emphasis on *experimenting* is significant: digital pedagogy is not a solved problem. The practitioner is a researcher of their own practice.

---

## 3.2 — Guidance

Using digital technologies to enhance interaction with learners, individually and collectively, inside and outside the session. Offering timely and targeted guidance and support. Developing new formats for that support.

The shift from scheduled instruction to ongoing guidance changes the temporal structure of the teacher-learner relationship.

---

## 3.3 — Collaborative Learning

Using digital technologies to foster learner collaboration. Enabling learners to use digital tools as part of collaborative work — for communication, cooperation, and collaborative knowledge creation.

See [[Cooperative Learning]] and [[Learning/Active Methodologies - Teaching and Learning/Frameworks/21st Century Skills]].

---

## 3.4 — Self-Regulated Learning

Using digital technologies to support learners' self-regulated learning: helping them plan, monitor, and reflect on their own learning; provide evidence of progress; share insights; and produce creative responses.

Self-regulation is both a competence and a disposition. [[Active Methodologies]] and [[Empowering Learners]] address this from different angles.
