A structured breathing regulation technique built on four equal phases. Also known as square breathing or tactical breathing.

---

## The Four Phases

**Inhale** — breathe in slowly through the nose for a count of four.

**Hold** — retain the breath for a count of four.

**Exhale** — release slowly through the mouth for a count of four.

**Hold** — pause at the bottom of the breath for a count of four.

Repeat for four to six cycles.

---

## Why It Works

The equal timing of all four phases activates the parasympathetic nervous system — the body's rest and regulation mode. The deliberate hold phases, in particular, interrupt the fast, shallow breathing pattern associated with anxiety and stress responses.

The counting provides a cognitive scaffold: the mind has something simple to do, which reduces the space available for anxious thought.

---

## Use in Learning Environments

Box breathing is suitable for use at the start of a session, before a high-stakes activity (presentation, assessment, public performance), or as a reset after a difficult moment. It takes approximately two minutes.

Used consistently, it teaches learners a self-regulatory tool they carry with them independently of any classroom or teacher.
