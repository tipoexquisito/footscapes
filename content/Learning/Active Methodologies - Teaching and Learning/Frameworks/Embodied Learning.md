Embodied learning is grounded in the idea that cognition is not confined to the brain. The body participates in knowing: posture, gesture, movement, sensation and physical experience all contribute to how we understand and remember.

Theoretically, embodied learning draws on phenomenological traditions (Merleau-Ponty), cognitive science (embodied cognition), and educational practice (drama education, Montessori, experiential learning).

In the course, embodied learning appeared in several forms:
- The [[Tableau]] technique, in which participants physically constructed frozen images to represent abstract ideas
- The [[Marzipan Time — A Mindfulness Exercise]], structured as a deliberate engagement of the senses
- Walking as pedagogy — 103 km over seven days, the city understood through movement
- The [[5-4-3-2-1 Grounding Exercise]], engaging body-sensation as a grounding tool

Embodied learning connects to [[Multisensory Learning]], [[Place-based Learning]] and [[Experiential Learning]].
