> "Dissemination means to spread widely."
> Erasmus+ Programme Guide

Dissemination in the Erasmus+ context is often described in bureaucratic terms — a reporting requirement, an output obligation. The Saturday session reframed it entirely.

Dissemination is not sharing results. It is **legitimising the work teachers do** — making visible what is otherwise closed inside classrooms and invisible to the world that depends on it.

> "Teachers make grandiose things but we're not aware." (Eduardo, course participant)

---

## What Dissemination Is Not

It is not:
- sharing deliverables
- fulfilling bureaucratic Erasmus+ requirements
- presenting products at the end of a project

These are the forms dissemination takes when it has been reduced to compliance. They are not its purpose.

---

## Dissemination vs Communication

Two related but distinct ideas:

**[[Learning/Active Methodologies - Teaching and Learning/Dissemination/Dissemination]]** — making practice available to the professional and scientific community. Peer-to-peer. Within education.

**Communication** — making education visible to civil society. Beyond the profession. Outward.

In most schools, professional knowledge circulates internally — among teachers, within the building, through documents no one outside reads. The course argued that education must become publicly visible. This is both a professional responsibility and a political act.

See [[Disseminare]] for the etymological and conceptual root.
