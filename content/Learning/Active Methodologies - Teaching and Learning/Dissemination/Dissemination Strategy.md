## What to Disseminate

> "Our experience of Europe — and of the world."

Not "an activity". A lived European experience. The 9+1 countries represented in the room — Bulgaria, Croatia, Portugal, France, Czech Republic, Germany, Italy, Slovakia, Greece, Spain — were not just a list of origins. They were Europe as a relational experience: co-presence, comparison, cultural negotiation, and shared pedagogical practice.

What is worth sharing is not the programme. It is the understanding that emerges from being in it.

---

## Who to Reach

Dissemination is most powerful when it crosses the usual boundaries of professional circulation.

Inside the school: students, staff, headteacher, school board, parent association.
Beyond the school: educational authorities, professional journals and websites, local radio, social media, the general public.

The "Do you have 5 minutes?" approach — a short, structured conversation with a colleague — is a low-barrier starting point. Not every dissemination act needs to be a conference presentation.

---

## How to Share

- Lessons that embed what was learned
- eTwinning and online community participation
- Conferences, seminars, school meetings
- E-newsletters, reports, articles
- Handbooks

One proposition that emerged: *Making a Handbook of Good Education in Europe* — a pedagogical manifesto in document form. To document practice is to produce professional culture.
