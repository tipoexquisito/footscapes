Educational change does not always begin with a conference or a publication. It begins in the corridor. In the staffroom. In a five-minute conversation.

---

## The Informal Dimension

The "Do you have 5 minutes?" approach is the most accessible form of dissemination: a structured, low-barrier conversation with a colleague about something you learned, something that worked, something worth sharing.

No slides required. No institutional approval. No audience larger than one.

---

## You Are the Intellectual

One of the session's most direct challenges to participants: many teachers do not think of themselves as producers of knowledge. They see themselves as curriculum executors — people who implement what others design.

The session pushed back on this. Hard.

A teacher who reflects carefully on their practice, documents what works, and shares it with others is doing something intellectually significant. The knowledge they produce is grounded in real classrooms, real learners, real constraints. It is not less valuable than academic research — it is differently valuable, and often more immediately useful.

> TikTok — Tic-tac — Tuk-tuk

Even said playfully, the point stands: contemporary dissemination demands new formats. A one-minute video, a social media post, a newsletter — these are legitimate channels for professional knowledge. Refusing to use them because they feel informal is how professional knowledge stays trapped in rooms it never leaves.
