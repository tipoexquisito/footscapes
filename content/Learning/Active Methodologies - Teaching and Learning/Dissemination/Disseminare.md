Italian for "to scatter seeds." The etymological root of the concept of [[Learning/Active Methodologies - Teaching and Learning/Dissemination/Dissemination]] in the Erasmus+ context — and the more honest word for what sharing professional practice actually means.

---

## What It Means

Disseminare is not:
- imposing
- centralising
- normalising

It is:
- scattering
- launching
- cultivating
- allowing growth

The metaphor matters. Seeds do not grow where they are thrown — they grow where conditions allow. Dissemination, understood this way, is an act of trust in others' contexts, not a delivery of conclusions.

---

## Rhizomatic Thinking

The metaphor connects to the Erasmus+ Spirit as a rhizomatic ecosystem. Small experiences of mobility circulate, connect, and transform other practices — not through direct transmission, but through the kind of lateral, unpredictable growth that rhizomes produce.

Disseminare is not broadcast. It is seeding.

---

## Pedagogical Implication

Dissemination understood this way is not bureaucratic compliance. It is civic and professional responsibility — making the invisible work of teachers visible to the world that benefits from it.
