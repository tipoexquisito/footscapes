**Fiat Corde** is a ten-minute talk built like a film, presented at an internal pedagogical conference, on the panel dedicated to [[Active Methodologies]]. It is not a mobility report. It is a compressed art history lesson whose thesis is the thesis of this garden: [[Context4Content|context does not illustrate content. Context is the content]].

The material comes straight from the week the talk describes: [[Goya]]'s *[[El 2 de mayo de 1808 en Madrid|2 de mayo]]* and *[[El 3 de mayo en Madrid|3 de mayo]]*, seen at the [[Highlights of Spanish Art|Prado]], carried through the rest of a week spent [[Images of Madrid|walking Madrid]] and stopping wherever the city was doing the teaching, including a night at [[Sala Equis]].

The deck is not a page of this garden. It is a separate, cinematic piece, built to be watched, not browsed: hard cuts, silence, one state at a time.

**[Watch the deck →](https://tipoexquisito.github.io/fiat-corde/)**

## What's next

A director's cut is planned: a version of the deck built to be browsed and re-watched on its own terms, once the live talk has been given and the pressure of a fixed ten minutes is gone.
