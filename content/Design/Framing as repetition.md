
# Framing as Repetition

> The *moldura* is not a limit. It is what creates the object.

Porto Design Biennale, in a short [Instagram piece](https://www.instagram.com/reel/DbBbUyNDaCp/?igsh=MXE5a3A4N2JqMGhkdQ==) on the making of the visual identity for *Time is Present — Designing the Common*, has creative director [[João Queirós]] ([[esad-idea]]) describing how the identity was built. 

Repetition became the core device, a way to echo the biennale's theme of shared, constant present. Queirós frames this repetition itself as a kind of [[framing]], not decoration but the structure that let the identity travel coherently across formats and materials.

Seven projects, seven colours. In the biennale's communication, these colours can appear alone, in pairs, in trios, or all together. Every combination is legitimate. Nothing is structurally excluded from appearing.

---

## Why this matters

This is [[FRAME+SHOT]] approached from the opposite direction. [[Derrida]]'s parergon argument, that the frame does not merely limit but constitutes the object, is here enacted in graphic design practice rather than philosophy or curation. The repetition is not illustrating the theme of the present. The repetition *is* the present, made visible through recurrence. [[Context4Content]] again, this time from the graphic design side rather than the pedagogical or curatorial one.

There is a genuine tension worth holding against [[Fiat Corde]] and the [[Director's Cut]]. The “Quintados sem pintor” thesis is about who is excluded from the frame, life not framed as grievable. Queirós describes a device built for the opposite: a frame engineered so that no combination is left out, where inclusion is systemic by design. Worth asking whether graphic design, when the frame is built well, can achieve a kind of structural inclusion that history painting never could. Or whether this is simply a different register of stakes, decorative pluralism versus the politics of who gets pictured at all.
