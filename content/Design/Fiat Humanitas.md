> And when we say "our water", and when we say "our seeds", and when Palestinians say "our land".

![[The 2nd of May 1808 in Madrid or “The Fight against the Mamelukes”.jpg]]
*Francisco de Goya y Lucientes, El 2 de mayo de 1808 en Madrid, 1814. Oil on canvas, 268 × 347 cm. Museo Nacional del Prado, Madrid. [museodelprado.es](https://www.museodelprado.es/en/the-collection/art-work/the-2nd-of-may-1808-in-madrid-or-the-fight/57dacf2e-5d10-4ded-85aa-9ff6f741f6b1)*

History reminds us that civilization is not something fixed in time or place. As Goya reported, and as history keeps confirming, there are no simple narratives of heroes and villains, winners and losers. A battle does not define a war. The interpretation of history always depends on perspective, memory and context.

History doesn't repeat itself. It rhymes.

![[The 3rd of May 1808 in Madrid, or “The Executions”.jpg]]
*Francisco de Goya y Lucientes, El 3 de mayo de 1808 en Madrid, 1814. Oil on canvas, 268 × 347 cm. Museo Nacional del Prado, Madrid. [museodelprado.es](https://www.museodelprado.es/en/the-collection/art-work/the-3rd-of-may-1808-in-madrid-or-the-executions/5e177409-2993-4240-97fb-847a02c6496c)*

So what does it mean to be an educator today?
And what will we need now and in the future?

Learning no longer happens exclusively within traditional classroom structures. It unfolds beyond the classroom: collaboratively, contextually, interdisciplinarily, engaging communication, collaboration, creativity and critical thinking. Today's educator needs a true ecosystem of tools, methodologies and languages capable of responding to the diversity of students' rhythms, needs and lived experiences.

In this process we may draw upon digital tools, art and craft, cities, museums, culture and lived experience itself. Knowledge grows through symbolism and dialogue, through encounters and through the dissemination of ideas, experiences and good practices shared from one person to another, from teachers to teachers, from minority communities to society itself: memory and visibility.

![Pablo Picasso, Guernica, 1937. Oil on canvas. Museo Nacional Centro de Arte Reina Sofía, Madrid](https://recursos.museoreinasofia.es/styles/large_landscape/public/Obra/DE00050_2.jpg.webp)
*Pablo Picasso, Guernica, 1937. Oil on canvas, 349 × 776 cm. Museo Nacional Centro de Arte Reina Sofía, Madrid. © Succession Picasso / VEGAP, Madrid. Image: Museo Reina Sofía, educational use.*

We must develop critical thinking. Learn to observe from multiple perspectives. Know how to immerse ourselves in the process and, equally, how to step back from it through reflection, self-observation and critical awareness.

Education is not merely the transmission of information.
It is the careful cultivation and sharing of meaning.

![African Guernica — Dumile Feni, 1967](https://recursos.museoreinasofia.es/styles/large_landscape/public/Exposiciones/Dumile-Feni-African-Guernica-escalado.jpg.webp)

Whatever the medium, what truly matters is being mindfully present. Not merely marking attendance. Learning requires us to activate the senses, respect individual rhythms and care for ourselves and others with empathy, openness and resilience. It also asks us to remain available to others: to communicate, to participate and to contribute actively to the collective construction and dissemination of knowledge.

Perhaps it is precisely within this coexistence of differences, memories and shared values that freedom can ultimately emerge.

![Mohammed Al-Hawajri, Guernica-Gaza, 2010–2013. Photomontage](https://www.mohammedalhawajri.com/images/plates/65e7df7fb1f7009adb60e8534c68a488.jpg)
*Mohammed Al-Hawajri, Guernica-Gaza, 2010–2013. Photomontage. © Mohammed Al-Hawajri. Image reproduced for educational reference. [mohammedalhawajri.com](https://www.mohammedalhawajri.com/)*

Because learning also means returning to places we have already passed through and discovering what we missed, what's changed. "To see in spring what was seen in summer, see by day what was seen at night", as José Saramago once wrote. To learn, unlearn and learn again with the openness, curiosity and imagination of a child.

The problem is, as Picasso reminded us, that the child grows up.

Perhaps this is why education, culture and dissemination matter so deeply. They allow us to keep scattering seeds of curiosity, humanity, dialogue and imagination into the world.

![Mohammed Al-Hawajri, Guernica-Gaza, 2010–2013. Photomontage](https://www.mohammedalhawajri.com/images/plates/fa615274caac7bef76445327029d494e.jpg)

*Fiat humanitas.*
