> What if problems don't need a solution?


The [[Footscapes]] garden is written in English. Deliberately, carefully, with attention to register and rhythm. The comprehension is there. The written production is there. But the spoken production under professional pressure, in an interview room, in a meeting with stakeholders who do not share your first language, that is where the trap closes.

Not because the ideas are absent. Because the words arrive slightly late. And in that gap between thought and utterance, what reads as hesitation is actually translation. The mind working in one language, the mouth performing in another.

[[Ludwig Wittgenstein]] might say these too are #LanguageTraps . The solution is not fluency as an abstract goal. It is precision in the specific vocabulary of the context you are trying to enter. *Talent development. Learning journey . Onboarding. Enablement. Stakeholder alignment.* Name the world you want to inhabit. The words will expand it.

---

The problems you have been carrying for years, the ones that felt too large, too complicated, too familiar to resolve.

> What if problems don't need a solution?
> What if they only need a better description?
