# Phenomenon-Based Learning

A Finnish curriculum design model. Instead of teaching separate disciplines in sequence and hoping students connect them later, teaching starts from a real, concrete phenomenon in the world, and lets disciplines cross through it, in whatever order and to whatever degree the phenomenon itself demands.

The unit of teaching is not the subject. It is the phenomenon. The question is not "what content gets covered today" but "what question about the world deserves asking, and which disciplines does that question pull in on its own".

[[Learning/Active Methodologies - Teaching and Learning/Context4content/index|Context4Content]], the framework introduced during the Erasmus+ [[Learning/Active Methodologies - Teaching and Learning/index|Active Methodologies for Teaching and Learning]] course by [[English Matters]], argues the same principle from a different angle: context does not illustrate content, context is the content. A phenomenon, a photograph, a museum wall, functions as the starting point that pulls everything else along with it.

The two frameworks arrived from different directions. Context4Content came out of a specific course, built around field learning projects in Madrid and Toledo. Phenomenon-based learning comes from an entire education system, Finland's, reorganised around the same underlying principle. Neither invented the idea. Each gives it a different scale of institutional backing, and each strengthens the case for the other.
