[Arco Maior](https://www.arcomaior.pt) operates within the PIEF (*Programa Integrado de Educação e Formação*), a state framework designed for young people at risk of school dropout or exclusion. Its tagline — *A cidade responde ao abandono escolar* — situates the institution in the urban fabric of Porto rather than in a remedial periphery.

Its pedagogical model is built on project-based learning, interdisciplinary integration and the [[ROAD - Roteiro de Objetivos de Aprendizagem e Desenvolvimento]] (*Roteiro de Objetivos de Aprendizagem e Desenvolvimento*) as a personal learning roadmap. Each student holds a ROAD: a structured document in which they define learning objectives per project, self-assess progress across disciplines and social-emotional competences, and receive teacher evaluation on a 1–4 scale. The ROAD travels with the student throughout each project and closes with a dual evaluation — the student's own (*A.F.*) alongside the pedagogical team's (*A.E.P.*) — before a final grade is agreed and signed.

Social-emotional competences are assessed through the [[CASEL]] framework, adapted by Arco Maior into three domains: *SER* (self-awareness and self-regulation, codes S1–S7), *CRESCER* (relational skills and decision-making, codes C1–C5) and *PERTENCER* (social competences and agency for change, codes P1–P3). These domains are not background values. They are formally assessed objectives, embedded in the daily activity record alongside disciplinary competences, session by session.

This is not a school that tolerates passivity. Learning here is understood as relational, embodied and purposeful.
