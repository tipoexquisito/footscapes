Neuromap is the working framework taught in the *UFCD de Neuromarketing*, the vocational course unit this project's [[Experience/CAO Visit Project|June 2026 visit]] to [[Centro de Arte Oliva]] served. It gives students a shared vocabulary for something they already do instinctively — decide, in seconds, whether an image, an advert or a room is worth their attention — by breaking that instinct into three layers, six levers and a four-step reading.

---

## Three Brains

Every decision is read through three registers, borrowed loosely from triune-brain models of persuasion rather than as literal neuroanatomy:

**Racional** — the register of argument, comparison, evidence.
**Emocional** — the register of feeling, association, memory.
**Instintivo** — the register of gut reaction, faster than either of the other two and first to respond.

The claim the framework makes is not that one register wins. It's that most persuasion aims first at the instinctive brain, then the emotional, and only last, if at all, at the rational one.

---

## Six Stimuli

Six levers a message can pull, individually or in combination:

**Centrado** — a direct address, a visible recipient, the sense of being spoken to.
**Contraste** — one thing set against another: before/after, analogue/digital, noise/silence.
**Tangível** — something concrete, sensory, held rather than only described.
**Princípio e fim** — the hook and the close: what opens a message and what it leaves behind.
**Visual** — image and composition doing the work language would otherwise have to do.
**Emocional** — the direct pull on feeling, collective memory, immediate reaction.

---

## Four Steps: Pain, Claim, Gain, Brain

A message is read, or built, through four moves in sequence:

**Pain** — what discomfort, lack or threat does this open with?
**Claim** — what does it assert in response to that pain?
**Gain** — what benefit follows if the claim is accepted?
**Brain** — which of the three brains, and which of the six stimuli, is actually doing the work of persuasion underneath the claim?

---

## Why this matters

Neuromap gave the [[Experience/CAO Visit Project|CAO visit]] its debrief structure. Three exhibitions in one day — [[Horror Vacui]], [[Sem Terra à Vista]], [[Zapping]] — could easily have stayed three disconnected museum experiences. Reading each of them back through Neuromap's four steps gave students one continuous instrument instead: a sculpture in *Horror Vacui*, a photograph in *Sem Terra à Vista* and a switched-off television in *Zapping* all became, briefly, the same kind of object — something built to move a viewer through pain, claim and gain, whether or not its maker used those words. The line that survived the whole day intact, borrowed from the *Zapping* núcleo Memória: *o medium mudou, o cérebro não* — the medium changed, the brain didn't.
