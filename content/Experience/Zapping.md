*Zapping: Televisão como cultura e contracultura* reads the language of broadcast media through a confrontation: the technological and cultural impact of television, mostly in its ephemeral, scheduled form, against its reflection and instrumentalisation as counterculture in artistic practice. The exhibition is structured in five *núcleos* marking major inflection points in television's socio-technical history — FRONTEIRA, RESOLUÇÃO, RECEÇÃO, COR and MEMÓRIA. Each núcleo pairs a field of RTP broadcast material, gathered around a theme, with a counter-field of countercultural artistic work engaging critically with that same technology and viewing experience.

The exhibition runs 7 February–28 June 2026, curated by Paula Parente Pinto, Alexandra Areias, Joaquim Moreno and Vera Carmo, and includes work by Alexandre Estrela, Ângela Ferreira, Helena Almeida, Nam June Paik, Martha Rosler, Dara Birnbaum and Wolf Vostell alongside RTP's own broadcast archive.

Three works anchored the group's visit, entering through the núcleo Memória: Nuno Nunes-Ferreira's *Zapping* (2025), 1,440 programmes compressed into 1,440 minutes, the archive itself made into the work; Rosa Baptista's *Maratona de TV* (2015/25), a fictional 1980s broadcast grid — the algorithm before the algorithm; and Antoni Muntadas's *La Televisión* (1980), a switched-off television set animated only by a projection of adverts and news, the medium as a corpse that keeps on communicating.

## Why this matters

This exhibition was seen in its final week — it closed five days after the visit — which is its own small lesson in the mediator's craft: reading a room that is about to disappear as though it were permanent, because for the ten students in front of it, it was. Originally designed as the most active stretch of the afternoon — autonomous group work, camera missions tied to each núcleo — it ended up being just the guided visit itself, the same scaling-back that met the [[The Disposable Camera|disposable camera]] elsewhere in the day.

The núcleo Fronteira makes the RTP/censorship thread explicit rather than implied: the exhibition's own journal traces Salazar's wary use of television as propaganda, Marcelo Caetano's *Conversas em Família* broadcasts, the "touristic" campaigns run alongside the Colonial War, and the military occupation of RTP's Lumiar studios at 3:06 a.m. on 25 April 1974 as the moment television became, briefly, an instrument of the revolution rather than the regime. This is the thread flagged as pending in [[Notes on Fiat Corde]] and in the earlier drafts behind [[Fiat Corde]] — Zapping/RTP/Estado Novo censorship as a parallel case of framing and control exercised through Portuguese state television, at national scale rather than the scale of a single photograph or a single classroom. It confirms the thread was worth flagging; connecting it in detail to what the group actually saw and discussed on 23 June remains undone.

Muntadas's dead television, still transmitting, is the same argument the day had already made twice before, at [[Horror Vacui]] and [[Sem Terra à Vista]], in a different material: that a medium does not need to be alive, or contemporary, or even switched on, to keep shaping what a room believes it is seeing.

What travelled cleanly from the exhibition into the [[Neuromap]] framework used to debrief the day: *o medium mudou, o cérebro não* — the medium changed, the brain didn't. Nunes-Ferreira's compressed archive and Baptista's fictional grid are both, in Neuromap's terms, exercises in **Frame** before they are anything else: someone decided what counted as a night of television, decades before an algorithm did it for free.
