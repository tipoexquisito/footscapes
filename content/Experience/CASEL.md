CASEL, the Collaborative for Academic, Social, and Emotional Learning, is an American research and advocacy organisation that developed the most widely used framework for social-emotional learning (SEL) in schools worldwide. Founded in 1994, its framework organises the competences students need to manage their own emotions, build relationships and make responsible decisions.

---

## The Five Core Competences

**Self-awareness** — recognising one's own emotions, values and strengths; understanding how thoughts and feelings influence behaviour.

**Self-management** — regulating emotions, managing stress, setting goals and persisting through difficulties.

**Social awareness** — understanding others' perspectives, including people from different backgrounds and cultures; recognising social and ethical norms.

**Relationship skills** — communicating, cooperating, negotiating conflict and resisting inappropriate social pressure; building and maintaining healthy relationships.

**Responsible decision-making** — making ethical, constructive choices about personal behaviour and social interactions, considering consequences for self and others.

## Arco Maior's Adaptation

[[Arco Maior]] uses CASEL as the assessment backbone of its pedagogical model, reorganised into three domains with individual competence codes:

**SER** (self and self-regulation): Autoconhecimento (S1–S4) and Autorregulação (S5–S7).
**CRESCER** (growing): Capacidades Relacionais (C1–C2) and Tomada de Decisões (C3–C5).
**PERTENCER** (belonging): Competências Sociais (P1–P2) and Agente de Mudança (P3).

Each project at Arco Maior formally maps activities to specific CASEL codes. These appear in the activity grid alongside disciplinary competences, session by session. The [[ROAD - Roteiro de Objetivos de Aprendizagem e Desenvolvimento]] records and evaluates student progress against them throughout each project.

> [!concept] CASEL competences at Arco Maior are not aspirational values — they are assessed objectives. The same codes that appear on a lesson plan appear on a student's final grade document.

## Evidence Base

Durlak et al. (2011) conducted a meta-analysis of 213 school-based SEL programmes involving over 270,000 students. Programmes following the CASEL framework produced an 11-percentile-point gain in academic achievement alongside measurable improvements in social-emotional skills, attitudes and behaviour, and reduced conduct problems.
