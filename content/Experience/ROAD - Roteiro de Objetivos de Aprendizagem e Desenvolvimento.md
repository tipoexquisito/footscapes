> The ROAD makes assessment visible to the student rather than done to them.


The ROAD — *Roteiro de Objetivos de Aprendizagem e Desenvolvimento* (Learning and Development Objectives Roadmap) — is the individual assessment tool used in every project at [[Arco Maior]]. It is not a test. It is a structured document that travels with each student throughout a project, recording learning objectives, progress and evaluations across both disciplinary and [[CASEL]] social-emotional competences.

---

## How It Works

At the start of each project, the student receives a ROAD specifying the competences to be assessed: one column per participating discipline, one section for CASEL social-emotional competences.

Throughout the project, the student uses the ROAD for self-assessment (*Avaliação do Formando*, A.F.) — rating their own performance against each competence. The pedagogical team conducts a parallel assessment (*Avaliação da Equipa Pedagógica*, A.E.P.). Both evaluations are recorded in the same document.

At project end, a final grade is agreed and signed by the student, then countersigned by the team coordinator. The ROAD becomes part of the student's portfolio.

> [!concept] The ROAD makes assessment visible to the student rather than done to them. The dual-column structure — self-assessment alongside teacher assessment — turns evaluation into a conversation rather than a verdict.

---

## What It Reveals

The gap between A.F. and A.E.P. scores is itself pedagogically significant. A student who consistently underrates their own performance reveals something different from one who consistently overrates it. The ROAD is a record of both the work and the student's relationship to their own work.
