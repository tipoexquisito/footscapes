*Horror Vacui*, curated by Marta Jecu, treats its title as a diagnostic tool rather than a fixed concept: a way of naming how cultures negotiate emptiness. Dozens of anthropomorphic figures from the [[Centro de Arte Oliva|Coleção Treger Saint Silvestre]] — animals, machines, spirits, humans, monsters, angels — crowd the rooms, anarchy and excess read here as mechanisms of protection against uncertainty. A significant part of the exhibition is a text the Portuguese writer Gonçalo M. Tavares wrote specifically for it, presented as a sound piece that resonates through every room and accompanies the visitor's own path through the work.

The exhibition runs from 21 March 2026 to 24 January 2027 and draws on outsider art from artists across Europe, the Americas, Africa and Asia — Aloïse, Rosa Ramalho, José de Guimarães, Reinata Sadimba and Serge Jolimeau among dozens of others.

---

## Why this matters

This was the first room the group entered on 23 June 2026, and its logic set the terms for the rest of the day: figures made without a market, a client, or an art-historical audience in mind, some of them anonymous, all of them insistently, excessively present.

The activity ran as designed: each student walked the rooms, chose one figure that affected them, and spent five minutes writing a short piece of storytelling from it, adapting the format of the FARSAS zine, anchored by a single load-bearing question: *was this work made to communicate with someone at all?* It is a deliberately unstable question in a room built from *arte bruta* — much of which resists exactly that expectation — and it is the same question the day would return to, in a different register, in front of Augusto Alves da Silva's photograph in [[Sem Terra à Vista]]: who is a work of art for, and what happens when the answer isn't obvious.
