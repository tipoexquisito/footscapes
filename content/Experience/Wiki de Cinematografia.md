> Three years of accurate notes. Nobody had ever asked what a reader was supposed to do with them.

The wiki started as personal reference material for Multimédia classes at Escola Secundária de Arouca — camera movements, shot scales, the five-phase production process, technique notes accumulated across years of teaching audiovisual production. By 2026 it had grown into something substantial: history and theory of cinema across seven pages, a database tracking the five phases from Development to Distribution, and a resource bank linking technique notes back to the phase where each one applies. The content was accurate. Some of it — a full crew hierarchy, a DSLR field guide, real storyboard examples from *Alien* and *Inception* — was genuinely good.

None of it had ever been asked to teach anyone anything on purpose.

---

## The Design Problem

A wiki like this one accumulates the way teaching notes always do: one page at a time, each one solving the problem in front of you that week, none of them designed against the others. Nobody had set a learning objective for any page. Nobody had asked a reader to do anything with what they had just read. Five pages existed only as a bare label in front of a database — no framing, no reason given for why the table mattered. A handful of small errors from years of nobody re-reading — a mistyped word, an autocorrected line that no longer meant anything — sat undisturbed because there was no audit loop built into the format. And several genuinely useful pages — a script-breakdown template, a shot-planning guide, a narrative-structures primer with its own worked exercise — lived several clicks deep inside "see more" callouts that only made sense if you already knew they were there.

This is the failure mode a well-organised reference collection falls into without anyone noticing: it rewards the person who already knows the territory and gives the newcomer no way in.

---

## The Redesign

The fix was not new content. It was instructional design applied retroactively to old content, in three moves.

**Objectives and retrieval.** Every core page — the seven history-and-theory pages, the five production phases — received a one-sentence learning objective at the top, pitched at what a reader should be able to *do* next rather than what the page covers, and a reflection question at the bottom. The pairing matters more than either half alone: the objective sets the target, the question forces retrieval rather than recognition. [[Vygotsky — Zone of Proximal Development|Vygotsky]]'s point about learning happening at the edge of current capacity applies to a wiki page the same way it applies to a lesson — an objective only works if it names something just past what the reader already has.

**A glossary as scaffolding.** Roughly thirty-five terms, scattered across the wiki with no shared entry point, were pulled into one page, each definition linked back to the page where it actually gets used. [[Scaffolding]] that withdraws on contact: a reader who already knows what *rack focus* means never has to visit it; a reader who doesn't has one click to a definition and one click back into context. The logic follows [[David Ausubel — Meaningful Learning|Ausubel]] more than it follows any glossary convention — a definition in isolation is memorised and lost; a definition tied to the page where the term is doing real work has somewhere to attach.

**A map for the pages nobody could find.** The five bare-database pages got a paragraph of framing before the table. The homepage got a full map of contents, surfacing every page that had been hiding inside a "see more" callout or a nested database — the same fix, at the level of the whole wiki rather than a single page.

---

## What the Redesign Was, Mechanically

The audit ran in two passes over the full wiki — structure first, then every page's actual content, including the pages that only surfaced on the second read. Corrections, the glossary, the objectives and the reflection questions were then written and applied directly, page by page, with an AI assistant doing the reading and the editing under direction, not unchecked. Each change was verified against the source page before being treated as done, and at least one ambiguous error — a word autocorrect had mangled past recovery — was flagged rather than silently guessed, and only corrected once the author supplied the word that had actually been intended. That is the same posture [[AI Literacy]] asks for: an assistant used for labour, not for authority — useful for surfacing years of accumulated detail a single re-read would have missed, not trusted to invent the meaning of a broken sentence it could not verify.
