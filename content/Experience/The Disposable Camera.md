Working name *zapping-cam*: a single-page web app (`app.html` + `config.js`), built from scratch for [[Experience/CAO Visit Project|the June 2026 CAO visit]], designed to be opened from a QR code with no installation and no account. It imitates a disposable film camera on purpose — a Fuji Superia colour filter over every shot, a fixed roll of 24 exposures, a counter that turns red for the last five — so that a phone already in every student's pocket behaves, for one afternoon, like a object with actual scarcity: you do not get the shot back, and you do not get another roll.

Each exposure was to be tied to a mission, one of six categories mapped directly onto [[Neuromap]]'s six stimuli:

**A · Centrado** (blue) — a direct address, a visible recipient, a message with no words.
**B · Contraste** (green) — analogue against digital, noise against silence, before against after.
**C · Visual** (orange) — an image with no words, a composition of screens, colour as the stimulus itself.
**D · Emocional** (pink) — an immediate reaction, collective memory, counterculture read as feeling.
**E · Princípio e fim** (purple) — the hook, the incomplete narrative, what a viewer is left with.
**X · Livre** (red) — the one frame nobody else in the room would have taken.

Photographs were to stay on the device, undeveloped in every sense, until a "reveal" session back at school — the students seeing their own roll for the first time together, the way a disposable camera actually works.

---

## Why it stayed in development

The app was not ready for 23 June 2026. Along with the five-hour activation block originally planned to introduce it, it did not run on the day. The visit used a paper observation script instead, and the mediators — Bruna Santos and Mariana Rocha, coordinated beforehand by Daniel Costa — worked directly from that script rather than from missions issued through the app.

This is not the end of the tool, only where its record currently stops. The build (`app.html`, `config.js`), the mission structure and the QR-code distribution model are designed and specified; publishing it to a live URL and generating the QR code are the two steps that remained undone when this visit ran. It belongs here as a project still in development, not a project that was tried and abandoned.
